# Cathedral

A gothic cathedral of golden-ratio pointed arches whose window tracery is
generated by a Wolfram cellular automaton: grown from the floor, bent by
dissonance, healed at resolutions, dripping ridge-beams, and eroded to dust like
a codex ‚Äî cool/occult through the body, warm-gold at the finale.

> Sidecar design doc convention: this file lives beside `Cathedral.java` and is
> the source of truth for design decisions and curation history. Mark any constant
> or behavior you could not visually verify with an inline `CURATE:` note so the
> review session can find them (grep for `CURATE:`).

## Original / inspiration

The hero pattern for **"Chrome Country"** (Oneohtrix Point Never / Daniel
Lopatin, *R Plus Seven*, 2013), song 5 of 6 in Jeff's *Communicating* piece ‚Äî
the **"acceptance"** beat: *accepting my father's strict religion for its
merits*, message *Beauty in the Occult*. This is not a recreation of a prior
screensaver; it is an original interpretation of Jeff's headline concept
("cathedral arches grow; flying buttress") crossed with the arc critique's
central fix (`00-overview-critique.md` gap #2): **make the uncanny/Wolfram
treatment the hero's DEFAULT rendering mode, not a secondary pattern.** The
cathedral is therefore visibly generated-wrong from the start ‚Äî the arches are
clean architecture but their tracery is filled by an elementary cellular
automaton rather than drawn, which is the light-translation of Lopatin's
"almost corny but twisted in unpleasant directions" TTS choir (the uncanny
hinge that is the whole point of the track).

Preserved signatures from the brief (`chrome-brief.md` ¬ß5-6):

- **Architecture that BUILDS** (arches/buttresses rising as the chords assemble),
  filling toward a full nave at the 4:05 finale.
- **Return, not generic transcendence** ‚Äî the **Heal** trigger is the concrete
  return-callback the critique demanded (the 1:43 HEAL moment; reuse of the
  HEAL motif from `temper`): a resolution that visibly completes and glows the
  arches rather than just decaying a glitch.
- **Beauty in the occult** ‚Äî cool/violet stone through the body, warm-gold only
  at the finale; the Wolfram tracery and the shortest-path hue lerp (which
  passes cool‚Üígold through magenta/red, not green) keep it strange-sacred, not
  kitsch.
- **Codex teardown** ‚Äî Jeff's "tear them down by rotating slices 90¬∞ as if a
  codex" is the **TearDown** trigger.
- **The empty valley** (3:20-4:00) ‚Äî when the structure recedes, a sparse field
  of slow, cold, red-shifted stars holds the darkness (Jeff's "center empty /
  red-shifted parallax stars").

## Rendering approach

- **Base class**: `ApotheneumPattern`. The pattern needs the full cube ring
  (all four buttressed walls as one 200-column wrap strip) plus the cube
  interior (the enclosing **nave**), plus the cylinder ‚Äî none of which
  `ApotheneumRasterPattern` (cube-face-only) can reach.
- **Surfaces**:
  - **Cube exterior ring** ‚Äî the four cathedral walls, one continuous arcade.
  - **Cube interior** ‚Äî the nave; an exact copy of the exterior
    (`copyCubeExterior()`) so the audience standing inside is enclosed by the
    same architecture (brief: "interior-face use so the audience feels enclosed
    by a nave").
  - **Cylinder exterior + interior** ‚Äî the ambulatory arcade; the cylinder runs
    its own independent CA and is copied to its interior
    (`copyCylinderExterior()`).
- **Geometry mapping**: two `SurfaceCanvas` buffers ‚Äî 200√ó45 for the cube ring,
  120√ó43 for the cylinder ‚Äî drawn per-pixel then `copyTo` the surfaces
  column-major. Y = 0 is the top row; height-from-floor `hb = (h-1) - y`.
- **Golden-ratio arcade (per face, self-similar)**: the cube ring is divided
  into `CUBE_FACES = 4` faces (one wall each, 50 cols); the cylinder into
  `CYL_BAYS = 3` bays (40 cols each). Each face carries one **main arch centered**
  on it, flanked symmetrically by side arches whose **widths shrink by the golden
  ratio** (`W_k = W0/PHI^k`). The `Arches` knob is a **continuous count** N of
  arches shown per face: odd N are all whole arches meeting the face edge exactly
  (N=3 ‚Üí exactly 3); even N add **half-arches at the two face edges** (N=4 ‚Üí 3
  whole + 2 halves); N sweeps continuously (`computeArchLayout` solves the main
  width `W0 = 1/S(m)`, `m = (N‚àí1)/2`, so the outer arch is revealed fractionally,
  pop-free). Two adjacent faces' edge halves meet at the seam to form a whole
  arch, so the ring still reads seamless.
- **Self-similar heights**: an arch centered at normalized face position `u_c`
  gets its apex from the **same lancet curve** a single arch ridge uses ‚Äî
  `apex(u_c) = springH + (fullApex‚àíspringH)¬∑(1‚àí|u_c|)^ARCH_EXP`. So the arch tips
  across a face trace one big arch: the main (center) reaches `fullApex`
  (`APEX_FRAC = 1.0` ‚Üí **top pixel row at Growth 1**), peaks fall off toward the
  edges, and at N=7 the face reads as a fractal of arches. Widths keep the golden
  ratio; heights follow the curve.
- **Per-pixel arch model** (all fractions CURATE): for column `x`, arch-local
  `norm ‚àà [0,1]` (0 center, 1 edges); `topH = springH + (archApex‚àíspringH)¬∑
  (1‚àínorm)^ARCH_EXP`, with `springH`/`fullApex` scaled by `growthShown`. **Growth
  is instant** ‚Äî `growthShown = Growth` directly, no easing ‚Äî so the knob maps
  straight to height. A pixel inside is **stone** (floor plinth, pier, central
  mullion, or within `RIB_ROWS` of the outline); otherwise **window void**, lit by
  the CA tracery (the `CA` knob scales its brightness **linearly, so CA=0 is fully
  off / windows dark**), else a dim stained-glass backing.
- **Shader-quality antialiasing** (`silhouetteCoverage`): the silhouette alpha is
  computed by an **`SS`√ó`SS` supersample** of the continuous `topH` field
  (interpolated between adjacent columns, ring-wrapped), not just a 1-px vertical
  feather. This feathers **all** edges ‚Äî apex, curved sides, and arch-to-arch
  valleys ‚Äî so growing arches blend inter-pixel like a shader. `Smooth` mixes the
  hard result toward the antialiased one (`cover = 1 ‚àí Smooth¬∑(1‚àícoverage)`);
  `Smooth=0` restores the hard/posterized silhouette. `topH` is computed in a
  first pass so the AA can read neighbors. Glitch tear-shear stays unsmoothed.
- **Tracery CA (smooth down-flow)**: `byte[height][width]` history per surface,
  row 0 = top; a generation is an elementary (Wolfram) CA over the ring (wrap
  neighborhood). New generations push at the top and flow downward one row per
  step; `step()` rotates array pointers (zero-alloc). Between steps a fractional
  `caPhase` **vertically interpolates** each cell toward the row above (and the
  top row toward a freshly-evolved `caIncoming` scratch row), scaled by `Smooth`,
  so the tracery slides down smoothly instead of stepping. `Smooth = 0` restores
  the binary/posterized look.
- **Buffers (all preallocated; zero-alloc render)**: `ringCanvas`/`ringWarp`,
  `cylCanvas`/`cylWarp`, the two `byte[][]` CA histories, six star arrays, and
  the per-frame color cache. The `*Warp` canvases are used only when the glitch
  or teardown is active (`warpCopy`); otherwise the base canvas is copied
  directly.
- **Door-column handling**: `SurfaceCanvas.copyTo` guards door-shortened
  columns via `column.points.length`, so canvas rows past a door column's
  length are dropped automatically.

## Audio mapping

`AudioReactive`, ticked first line of `render()`, gated by the **Audio depth
knob** (`audio.setDepth(audioDepth)`):

- **level** (smoothed) ‚Äî global glow lift: adds up to +0.30 to the stone/tracery
  brightness `glow`, a gentle bloom with the organ swell.
- **treble** (smoothed) ‚Äî lifts tracery brightness up to +40%, so hissy/bright
  passages make the windows sparkle.
- **trebleHit** ‚Äî nudges one extra CA generation, a small shimmer on transients.

**Depth knob / silence behavior**: `Audio` defaults to 0 = pure screensaver. At
depth 0 (true silence, or the knob down) all magnitude taps read 0 and hits
never fire: the CA free-runs purely on its timer, glow rests at its
growth-driven baseline, and the whole build/heal/teardown grammar runs
identically without audio. Raising the knob to 1 adds the glow bloom, tracery
sparkle, and transient CA nudges described above. For this beatless track the
composition drives the arc through the **Growth / Gold / Glitch** knobs
(arrange automation from the MIR envelopes) and modest Audio depth for glow ‚Äî
not a 16th grid.

## Tempo mapping

This is a **beatless** track (brief: "Do not TempoLock this song"), so there is
no `Sync` control ‚Äî the CA always free-runs on an internal timer whose interval
is **`CASpd`-scaled** (`CA_STEP_AMBIENT_MS`‚Üí`CA_STEP_PEAK_MS`), currently in
absolute ms. The **`TempoDiv`** knob is the single tempo hook and it now drives
three musically-timed envelopes over one division period (`tempoLock.divisionMs`):

- **Bend** and **Heal** are **pulses**: a smooth onset over the first
  `ATTACK_FRAC` (25%) of the period, then a slower smooth fade over the remaining
  75% (`pulse()` = two `smoothstep` legs). Heal first snaps any in-flight Bend to
  zero, then blooms.
- **TearDown erosion** advances linearly over the period, then its visible
  progress is eased with `smoothstep` (`tearPhase = smoothstep(tearRaw)`) so the
  collapse and dust build settle in and out.

Growth, drip and stars are timer/parameter driven and never touch the grid.

## Triggers

Five triggers, small ‚Üí large:

- `seed` (small) ‚Äî the CA reseeds into a new fractal; **also restores** the
  cathedral if it has been eroded to dust (clears `destroyed`/`eroding`).
- `randomRule` (small) ‚Äî random-walk to a new curated CA rule that never
  revisits the last `RULES.length/2` (=5) chosen rules nor the current one.
- `bend` (medium) ‚Äî a dissonance glitch **pulse over one TempoDiv period**: a
  smooth swell for the first 25% (`ATTACK_FRAC`), then a slower smooth fade for
  the remaining 75% (two `smoothstep` legs). The image hue-shifts and tear-shears
  (the shear stays unsmoothed ‚Äî Jeff likes the crunch). CURATE: verify it reads as
  a "wrong" bend, not just noise.
- `heal` (medium/large) ‚Äî first snaps any in-flight Bend to zero, then runs the
  **same pulse shape** as a completing glow (warms color via `healGlow`). The
  concrete **return/homecoming callback** (1:43).
- `tearDown` (large) ‚Äî the codex teardown, a **one-time, time-lapse erosion**:
  over one `TempoDiv` period (progress eased by `smoothstep`), stone/CA pixels
  crumble away top-down ‚Äî normalized to **each arch's own height**, so the small
  side arches erode in step with the tall main arch ‚Äî while a **domed dust pile**
  builds along the floor (every-other-pixel checkerboard). At completion the
  cathedral stays `destroyed` (only dust renders) until `Seed` restores it.
  Ignored while eroding or already dust.

## Drip (ridge energy)

`drip` is bidirectional (‚àí1..1); its magnitude is eased/held in `dripEnergy` (a
one-pole build toward `|Drip|`, `DRIP_TAU`). Energy is painted **only on the arch
ridge/outline stone and the beam above it ‚Äî never into the windows**.

- **Color rules** (`dripPixel(intensity)`): the palette **warm/right hue**
  (`baseGold*`), with brightness varying across the form and the **brightest
  areas desaturating toward `DRIP_HOT_SAT` (20%)** ‚Äî hot near-white peaks over a
  gold body. Shared by the ridge traces and the beam particles.
- **Center-out ridge traces**: the traveling wave is parameterized by `colNorm`
  (0 at each arch's peak column, 1 at its edges), so it moves **down/out from the
  peak** for negative Drip and **in/up toward the peak** for positive ‚Äî following
  the arch, not sliding horizontally across the ring.
- **Ascending particle beam** (positive Drip): each arch peak emits **1‚Äì5 large
  particles** (`MAX_PARTICLES`), count scaling with `dripEnergy`, each with its
  own **rise speed** (`particleSpeed[]`, ~2‚Äì6 s to traverse). A particle rises
  from the peak up to **1√ó installation height** and up to **¬º arch width** wide,
  fading as it ascends. `Smooth` controls sub-pixel placement: at 1 the blob
  center tracks the fractional row (smooth ascent); at 0 it snaps to a row.

CURATE: `RIDGE_WAVE_K`, particle speeds/count, `PARTICLE_VRAD`, `DRIP_HOT_SAT`.

## Simulation-principles compliance

- **Growth (instant height)** ‚Äî Jeff's directive: Growth maps directly to
  height and responds instantly (`growthShown = Growth`, no easing). Sustained
  motion during a build comes from the **arrange automation ramping Growth**, not
  from an internal cap; the composition owns the raise rate.
- **CA tracery flow (sustained motion)** ‚Äî one row per step; free-run step
  interval 1.2 s (peak) ‚Üí 3.5 s (ambient), so a full-height (45-row) traversal
  is ‚â•54 s. Well above the cap.
- **Bend glitch (event-like)** ‚Äî a tempo pulse over one `TempoDiv` period (25%
  smooth onset, 75% fade); a transient distortion, not a traversal.
- **Heal glow (event-like)** ‚Äî the same tempo pulse; no spatial traversal.
- **TearDown (one-time erosion)** ‚Äî a single smoothstep-eased collapse over one
  `TempoDiv` period (default WHOLE), leaving a static dust pile until Seed. Not a
  looping motion; a deliberate destroy event.
- **Starfield** ‚Äî slow twinkle (period ~10 s) + very slow parallax drift; no
  fast motion.
- **Bold forms / contrast** ‚Äî stone ribs/piers/mullions with a dim window field
  and CA tracery. The `Smooth` knob controls antialiasing: at 1 the tracery
  crossfades smoothly as it flows down and arch edges are antialiased; at 0 it
  reverts to the hard/posterized binary look. Depth comes from the growth reveal.
  CURATE: verify the tracery reads as intentional generated tracery, not noise.
