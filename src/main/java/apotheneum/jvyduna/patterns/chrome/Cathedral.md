# Cathedral

A gothic cathedral of golden-ratio pointed arches whose window tracery is
generated by a Wolfram cellular automaton: grown from the floor, bent by
dissonance, healed at resolutions, dripping ridge-beams, and eroded to dust like
a codex — cool/occult through the body, warm-gold at the finale.

> Sidecar design doc convention: this file lives beside `Cathedral.java` and is
> the source of truth for design decisions and curation history. Mark any constant
> or behavior you could not visually verify with an inline `CURATE:` note so the
> review session can find them (grep for `CURATE:`).

## Original / inspiration

The hero pattern for **"Chrome Country"** (Oneohtrix Point Never / Daniel
Lopatin, *R Plus Seven*, 2013), song 5 of 6 in Jeff's *Communicating* piece —
the **"acceptance"** beat: *accepting my father's strict religion for its
merits*, message *Beauty in the Occult*. This is not a recreation of a prior
screensaver; it is an original interpretation of Jeff's headline concept
("cathedral arches grow; flying buttress") crossed with the arc critique's
central fix (`00-overview-critique.md` gap #2): **make the uncanny/Wolfram
treatment the hero's DEFAULT rendering mode, not a secondary pattern.** The
cathedral is therefore visibly generated-wrong from the start — the arches are
clean architecture but their tracery is filled by an elementary cellular
automaton rather than drawn, which is the light-translation of Lopatin's
"almost corny but twisted in unpleasant directions" TTS choir (the uncanny
hinge that is the whole point of the track).

Preserved signatures from the brief (`chrome-brief.md` §5-6):

- **Architecture that BUILDS** (arches/buttresses rising as the chords assemble),
  filling toward a full nave at the 4:05 finale.
- **Return, not generic transcendence** — the **Heal** trigger is the concrete
  return-callback the critique demanded (the 1:43 HEAL moment; reuse of the
  HEAL motif from `temper`): a resolution that visibly completes and glows the
  arches rather than just decaying a glitch.
- **Beauty in the occult** — cool/violet stone through the body, warm-gold only
  at the finale; the Wolfram tracery and the shortest-path hue lerp (which
  passes cool→gold through magenta/red, not green) keep it strange-sacred, not
  kitsch.
- **Codex teardown** — Jeff's "tear them down by rotating slices 90° as if a
  codex" is the **TearDown** trigger.
- **The empty valley** (3:20-4:00) — when the structure recedes, a sparse field
  of slow, cold, red-shifted stars holds the darkness (Jeff's "center empty /
  red-shifted parallax stars").

## Rendering approach

- **Base class**: `ApotheneumPattern`. The pattern needs the full cube ring
  (all four buttressed walls as one 200-column wrap strip) plus the cube
  interior (the enclosing **nave**), plus the cylinder — none of which
  `ApotheneumRasterPattern` (cube-face-only) can reach.
- **Surfaces**:
  - **Cube exterior ring** — the four cathedral walls, one continuous arcade.
  - **Cube interior** — the nave; an exact copy of the exterior
    (`copyCubeExterior()`) so the audience standing inside is enclosed by the
    same architecture (brief: "interior-face use so the audience feels enclosed
    by a nave").
  - **Cylinder exterior + interior** — the ambulatory arcade; the cylinder runs
    its own independent CA and is copied to its interior
    (`copyCylinderExterior()`).
- **Geometry mapping**: two `SurfaceCanvas` buffers — 200×45 for the cube ring,
  120×43 for the cylinder — drawn per-pixel then `copyTo` the surfaces
  column-major. Y = 0 is the top row; height-from-floor `hb = (h-1) - y`.
- **Golden-ratio arcade (per face, self-similar)**: the cube ring is divided
  into `CUBE_FACES = 4` faces (one wall each, 50 cols); the cylinder into
  `CYL_BAYS = 3` bays (40 cols each). Each face carries one **main arch centered**
  on it, flanked symmetrically by side arches whose **widths shrink by the golden
  ratio** (`W_k = W0/PHI^k`). The `Arches` knob is a **continuous count** N of
  arches shown per face: odd N are all whole arches meeting the face edge exactly
  (N=3 → exactly 3); even N add **half-arches at the two face edges** (N=4 → 3
  whole + 2 halves); N sweeps continuously (`computeArchLayout` solves the main
  width `W0 = 1/S(m)`, `m = (N−1)/2`, so the outer arch is revealed fractionally,
  pop-free). Two adjacent faces' edge halves meet at the seam to form a whole
  arch, so the ring still reads seamless.
- **Self-similar heights**: an arch centered at normalized face position `u_c`
  gets its apex from the **same lancet curve** a single arch ridge uses —
  `apex(u_c) = springH + (fullApex−springH)·(1−|u_c|)^ARCH_EXP`. So the arch tips
  across a face trace one big arch: the main (center) reaches `fullApex`
  (`APEX_FRAC = 1.0` → **top pixel row at Growth 1**), peaks fall off toward the
  edges, and at N=7 the face reads as a fractal of arches. Widths keep the golden
  ratio; heights follow the curve.
- **Per-pixel arch model** (all fractions CURATE): for column `x`, arch-local
  `norm ∈ [0,1]` (0 center, 1 edges); `topH = springH + (archApex−springH)·
  (1−norm)^ARCH_EXP`, with `springH`/`fullApex` scaled by `growthShown`. **Growth
  is instant** — `growthShown = Growth` directly, no easing — so the knob maps
  straight to height. The silhouette top edge is **antialiased** (coverage
  `clamp01((topH−hb)+0.5)`, softened by the `Smooth` knob). A pixel inside is
  **stone** (floor plinth, pier, central mullion, or within `RIB_ROWS` of the
  outline); otherwise **window void**, lit by the CA tracery, else a dim
  stained-glass backing.
- **Tracery CA (smooth down-flow)**: `byte[height][width]` history per surface,
  row 0 = top; a generation is an elementary (Wolfram) CA over the ring (wrap
  neighborhood). New generations push at the top and flow downward one row per
  step; `step()` rotates array pointers (zero-alloc). Between steps a fractional
  `caPhase` **vertically interpolates** each cell toward the row above (and the
  top row toward a freshly-evolved `caIncoming` scratch row), scaled by `Smooth`,
  so the tracery slides down smoothly instead of stepping. `Smooth = 0` restores
  the binary/posterized look.
- **Buffers (all preallocated; zero-alloc render)**: `ringCanvas`/`ringWarp`,
  `cylCanvas`/`cylWarp`, the two `byte[][]` CA histories, six star arrays, and
  the per-frame color cache. The `*Warp` canvases are used only when the glitch
  or teardown is active (`warpCopy`); otherwise the base canvas is copied
  directly.
- **Door-column handling**: `SurfaceCanvas.copyTo` guards door-shortened
  columns via `column.points.length`, so canvas rows past a door column's
  length are dropped automatically.

## Audio mapping

`AudioReactive`, ticked first line of `render()`, gated by the **Audio depth
knob** (`audio.setDepth(audioDepth)`):

- **level** (smoothed) — global glow lift: adds up to +0.30 to the stone/tracery
  brightness `glow`, a gentle bloom with the organ swell.
- **treble** (smoothed) — lifts tracery brightness up to +40%, so hissy/bright
  passages make the windows sparkle.
- **trebleHit** — nudges one extra CA generation, a small shimmer on transients.

**Depth knob / silence behavior**: `Audio` defaults to 0 = pure screensaver. At
depth 0 (true silence, or the knob down) all magnitude taps read 0 and hits
never fire: the CA free-runs purely on its timer, glow rests at its
growth-driven baseline, and the whole build/heal/teardown grammar runs
identically without audio. Raising the knob to 1 adds the glow bloom, tracery
sparkle, and transient CA nudges described above. For this beatless track the
composition drives the arc through the **Growth / Warmth / Glitch** knobs
(arrange automation from the MIR envelopes) and modest Audio depth for glow —
not a 16th grid.

## Tempo mapping

This is a **beatless** track (brief: "Do not TempoLock this song"), so there is
no `Sync` control — the CA always free-runs on an internal timer whose interval
is **`Speed`-scaled** (`CA_STEP_AMBIENT_MS`→`CA_STEP_PEAK_MS`), currently in
absolute ms. The only tempo hook is **`TempoDiv`**, which paces the one-time
**TearDown erosion**: the erosion completes over one `TempoDiv` period
(`tempoLock.divisionMs`), so the collapse is musically timed. Growth, glitch/heal
envelopes, drip and stars are all timer/parameter driven and never touch the grid.

## Speed units — DECISION NEEDED (Chrome outlier)

Chrome is a **per-song outlier**: part tempo-grid-driven (TearDown), part
deserving continuous speeds. Per Jeff, each Cathedral speed-ish param needs his
per-param call before converting to the grid-less **beat-relative** convention
used for Aumakua/PulseOrb. **Nothing was changed here pending that input** — the
CA advance is still absolute ms. Options per param:

| Param | What it drives | (a) beat-relative live-tempo | (b) tempo-grid (division) | (c) leave absolute |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `speed` | CA advance step interval (`CA_STEP_*_MS`) | continuous rows/beat like Aumakua | snap step to a `TempoDiv` | keep absolute ms (current) |
| `tempoDiv` (TearDown) | erosion duration | — | **keep** (already grid; the intended use) | — |
| Growth ease | 0→1 nave raise time | possible | — | keep absolute (current) |

## Speed mapping

| Quantity | Ambient (Speed=0) | Peak (Speed=1) | Curve |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth ease time (full 0→1 raise of the nave) | 8 s | 5 s | lin |
| CA free-run step interval | 3.5 s | 1.2 s | exp |

Sustained motion respects the ≥5 s full-traversal cap even at Speed=1: the growth
ease is rate-capped to never complete a 0→1 raise faster than 5 s, and the CA
flows one row per step (≥45 rows per full-height traversal = ≥54 s at peak).

## Triggers

Five triggers, small → large:

- `seed` (small) — the CA reseeds into a new fractal; **also restores** the
  cathedral if it has been eroded to dust (clears `destroyed`/`eroding`).
- `randomRule` (small) — random-walk to a new curated CA rule that never
  revisits the last `RULES.length/2` (=5) chosen rules nor the current one.
- `bend` (medium) — a dissonance glitch pulse: the image hue-shifts and
  tear-shears horizontally, then heals over ~2 s. CURATE: verify the shear reads
  as a "wrong" bend, not just noise.
- `heal` (medium/large) — the glitch snaps to zero and a completing glow floods
  the arches over ~3 s (also warms color via `healGlow`). The concrete
  **return/homecoming callback** (1:43).
- `tearDown` (large) — the codex teardown, now a **one-time, time-lapse
  erosion**: over one `TempoDiv` period, stone/tracery pixels crumble away
  top-down (rising-threshold hash noise) while a **domed dust pile** builds along
  the floor (every-other-pixel checkerboard). At completion the cathedral stays
  `destroyed` (only dust renders) until `Seed` restores it. Ignored while eroding
  or already dust. CURATE: erosion bias + dust dome.

## Drip (ridge energy)

`drip` is bidirectional (−1..1); its magnitude is eased/held in `dripEnergy` (a
one-pole build toward `|Drip|`, `DRIP_TAU`). Energy is painted **only on the arch
ridge/outline stone — never into the windows**. Negative Drip flows a
scintillating coating **down the ribs**; positive Drip gathers energy at each
arch peak and **builds it upward into a flowing beam** (up to **1× installation
height** above the peak, clipped at the canvas top, and up to **¼ the arch
width** wide, narrowing/tapering upward). Painted with `setMax` in `dripColor`
(bright gold). CURATE: scintillation rate, beam taper, brightness.

## Simulation-principles compliance

- **Growth (instant height)** — Jeff's directive: Growth maps directly to
  height and responds instantly (`growthShown = Growth`, no easing). Sustained
  motion during a build comes from the **arrange automation ramping Growth**, not
  from an internal cap; the composition owns the raise rate.
- **CA tracery flow (sustained motion)** — one row per step; free-run step
  interval 1.2 s (peak) → 3.5 s (ambient), so a full-height (45-row) traversal
  is ≥54 s. Well above the cap. Sync-on steps land on the grid but are still one
  row per crossing.
- **Bend glitch (event-like)** — ~2.2 s decay, ≥1.5 s event minimum; the shear
  is a transient distortion, not a traversal.
- **Heal glow (event-like)** — ~3 s settle, ≥1.5 s; no spatial traversal.
- **TearDown (one-time erosion)** — a single time-lapse collapse over one
  `TempoDiv` period (default WHOLE), leaving a static dust pile until Seed. Not a
  looping motion; a deliberate destroy event.
- **Starfield** — slow twinkle (period ~10 s) + very slow parallax drift; no
  fast motion.
- **Bold forms / contrast** — stone ribs/piers/mullions with a dim window field
  and CA tracery. The `Smooth` knob controls antialiasing: at 1 the tracery
  crossfades smoothly as it flows down and arch edges are antialiased; at 0 it
  reverts to the hard/posterized binary look. Depth comes from the growth reveal.
  CURATE: verify the tracery reads as intentional generated tracery, not noise.

  `ARCH_EXP` 0.6 (used for both each arch and the master peak-height envelope),
  `RIB_ROWS` 2, `BASE_ROWS` 2, `PIER_NORM` 0.82, `MULLION_NORM` 0.06, `PHI`
  golden ratio, `CUBE_FACES` 4, `CYL_BAYS` 3 — confirm the golden-ratio arcade

  off each arch's pier edges (from the peak arrays). Cube only; cylinder skips.

  (H52/S85/B92); full-Gold tracery target `YELLOW` (H52/S90/**B50**),
  `GOLD_STONE_BOOST` 0.35, `WINDOW_BACK` 0.10. Palette cache: swatch[0]=cool,
  swatch[last]=gold, **swatch[1]=CA tracery**. Confirm the split (arches brighter
  deep-gold vs. tracery dimmer yellow) reads at full Gold.
- Drip: `DRIP_TAU` 450 ms build; ridge coating 0.35+0.35·scint; beams to 1×

  sin-hash — confirm the bend reads as dissonance. (Glitch AA deferred — "not
