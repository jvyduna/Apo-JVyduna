# Cathedral

A gothic cathedral of pointed arches whose window tracery is generated by a
Wolfram cellular automaton: grown from envelope swells, bent by dissonance,
healed at resolutions, and torn down like a codex — cool/occult through the
body, warm-gold at the finale.

> Sidecar design doc convention: this file lives beside `Cathedral.java` and is
> the source of truth for design decisions and curation history. Mark any constant
> or behavior you could not visually verify with an inline `CURATE:` note so the
> review session can find them (grep for `CURATE:`).

## Original / inspiration

The hero pattern for **"Chrome Country"** (Oneohtrix Point Never / Daniel
Lopatin, *R Plus Seven*, 2013), song 5 of 6 in Jeff's *Communicating* piece —
the **"acceptance"** beat: *accepting my father's strict religion for its
merits*, message *Beauty in the Occult*. This is not a recreation of a prior
screensaver; it is an original interpretation of Jeff's headline concept
("cathedral arches grow; flying buttress") crossed with the arc critique's
central fix (`00-overview-critique.md` gap #2): **make the uncanny/Wolfram
treatment the hero's DEFAULT rendering mode, not a secondary pattern.** The
cathedral is therefore visibly generated-wrong from the start — the arches are
clean architecture but their tracery is filled by an elementary cellular
automaton rather than drawn, which is the light-translation of Lopatin's
"almost corny but twisted in unpleasant directions" TTS choir (the uncanny
hinge that is the whole point of the track).

Preserved signatures from the brief (`chrome-brief.md` §5-6):

- **Architecture that BUILDS** (arches/buttresses rising as the chords assemble),
  filling toward a full nave at the 4:05 finale.
- **Return, not generic transcendence** — the **Heal** trigger is the concrete
  return-callback the critique demanded (the 1:43 HEAL moment; reuse of the
  HEAL motif from `temper`): a resolution that visibly completes and glows the
  arches rather than just decaying a glitch.
- **Beauty in the occult** — cool/violet stone through the body, warm-gold only
  at the finale; the Wolfram tracery and the shortest-path hue lerp (which
  passes cool→gold through magenta/red, not green) keep it strange-sacred, not
  kitsch.
- **Codex teardown** — Jeff's "tear them down by rotating slices 90° as if a
  codex" is the **TearDown** trigger.
- **The empty valley** (3:20-4:00) — when the structure recedes, a sparse field
  of slow, cold, red-shifted stars holds the darkness (Jeff's "center empty /
  red-shifted parallax stars").

## Rendering approach

- **Base class**: `ApotheneumPattern`. The pattern needs the full cube ring
  (all four buttressed walls as one 200-column wrap strip) plus the cube
  interior (the enclosing **nave**), plus the cylinder — none of which
  `ApotheneumRasterPattern` (cube-face-only) can reach.
- **Surfaces**:
  - **Cube exterior ring** — the four cathedral walls, one continuous arcade.
  - **Cube interior** — the nave; an exact copy of the exterior
    (`copyCubeExterior()`) so the audience standing inside is enclosed by the
    same architecture (brief: "interior-face use so the audience feels enclosed
    by a nave").
  - **Cylinder exterior + interior** — the ambulatory arcade; the cylinder runs
    its own independent CA and is copied to its interior
    (`copyCylinderExterior()`).
- **Geometry mapping**: two `SurfaceCanvas` buffers — 200×45 for the cube ring,
  120×43 for the cylinder — drawn per-pixel then `copyTo` the surfaces
  column-major. Y = 0 is the top row; height-from-floor `hb = (h-1) - y`.
- **Arcade tiling**: the `Arches` knob is quoted per reference wall
  (`REF_WALL_COLS = 50`). Each ring is retiled to a **whole** number of arches
  `nRing = round(width · Arches / 50)` with `archWf = width/nRing`, so the
  arcade wraps seamlessly around the ring with no seam at column 0 (12 arches on
  the cube at Arches=3, ~7 on the cylinder). The four physical cube walls are
  not treated separately — a continuous seamless arcade reads better and dodges
  the per-face axis choice.
- **Per-pixel arch model** (all fractions CURATE): for column `x`, arch-local
  `norm ∈ [0,1]` (0 center, 1 edges); the pointed-arch outline height is
  `topH(norm) = springH + (apexH−springH)·(1−norm)^ARCH_EXP`, with `springH`
  and `apexH` both scaled by `growthShown` so the whole arch **grows from the
  floor**. A pixel inside the silhouette is **stone** if it is the floor plinth,
  a pier (edges, below springline), the central mullion, or within `RIB_ROWS`
  of the outline; otherwise it is **window void**, lit by the CA tracery where
  the automaton cell is set, else a dim stained-glass backing.
- **Tracery CA**: `byte[height][width]` history per surface, row 0 = top; a
  generation is an elementary (Wolfram) CA over the ring (wrap neighborhood).
  New generations are pushed at the top and flow downward one row per step,
  giving a slow shimmer. `step()` rotates array pointers (zero-alloc).
- **Buffers (all preallocated; zero-alloc render)**: `ringCanvas`/`ringWarp`,
  `cylCanvas`/`cylWarp`, the two `byte[][]` CA histories, six star arrays, and
  the per-frame color cache. The `*Warp` canvases are used only when the glitch
  or teardown is active (`warpCopy`); otherwise the base canvas is copied
  directly.
- **Door-column handling**: `SurfaceCanvas.copyTo` guards door-shortened
  columns via `column.points.length`, so canvas rows past a door column's
  length are dropped automatically.

## Audio mapping

`AudioReactive`, ticked first line of `render()`, gated by the **Audio depth
knob** (`audio.setDepth(audioDepth)`):

- **level** (smoothed) — global glow lift: adds up to +0.30 to the stone/tracery
  brightness `glow`, a gentle bloom with the organ swell.
- **treble** (smoothed) — lifts tracery brightness up to +40%, so hissy/bright
  passages make the windows sparkle.
- **trebleHit** — nudges one extra CA generation, a small shimmer on transients.

**Depth knob / silence behavior**: `Audio` defaults to 0 = pure screensaver. At
depth 0 (true silence, or the knob down) all magnitude taps read 0 and hits
never fire: the CA free-runs purely on its timer, glow rests at its
growth-driven baseline, and the whole build/heal/teardown grammar runs
identically without audio. Raising the knob to 1 adds the glow bloom, tracery
sparkle, and transient CA nudges described above. For this beatless track the
composition drives the arc through the **Growth / Warmth / Glitch** knobs
(arrange automation from the MIR envelopes) and modest Audio depth for glow —
not a 16th grid.

## Tempo mapping

The one grid-lockable motion is **CA generation stepping**. With `Sync` on,
steps fire on `TempoDiv` crossings (`tempoLock.crossed`, polled unconditionally
every frame). With `Sync` **off** — the intended mode for this **beatless**
song (brief: "Do not TempoLock this song") — steps free-run on an internal
timer whose interval is energy-scaled (`CA_STEP_AMBIENT_MS`→`CA_STEP_PEAK_MS`).
Growth, glitch/heal envelopes, teardown and stars are all timer/parameter
driven and never touch the grid, so the pattern is fully functional and good
looking with Sync off. No `retime()` clamp overrides are used (the CA step is a
discrete event gate, not a rate nudge).

## Energy mapping

| Quantity | Ambient (e=0) | Peak (e=1) | Curve |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth ease time (full 0→1 raise of the nave) | 8 s | 5 s | lin |
| CA free-run step interval (Sync off) | 3.5 s | 1.2 s | exp |

Sustained motion respects the ≥5 s full-traversal cap even at e=1: the growth
ease is rate-capped to never complete a 0→1 raise faster than 5 s, and the CA
flows one row per step (≥45 rows per full-height traversal = ≥54 s at peak).

## Triggers

Four non-meta triggers, small → large:

- `seed` (small) — the CA reseeds; the window tracery quietly regenerates into a
  new fractal. No structural change.
- `bend` (medium) — a dissonance glitch pulse: the whole image hue-shifts and
  tear-shears horizontally, then heals back to clean over ~2 s. Reads as the
  1:50 / 2:05 dissonance peaks bending the holy image. CURATE: verify the shear
  reads as a "wrong" bend, not just noise.
- `heal` (medium/large) — the glitch snaps to zero and a completing glow floods
  the arches over ~3 s (also warms the color via `healGlow`). The concrete
  **return/homecoming callback** (1:43). Resolution is the emotional event, so
  this is a visible completion, not a decay.
- `tearDown` (large) — the codex teardown: vertical slices fold toward their
  left page-edges in a staggered sweep, hold ~1 s, then rebuild (~3.5 s each
  leg). Ignored while one is already running. CURATE: the "rotate 90°" look is a
  first-pass page-fold approximation.

Rows mirror the `bag.jumpable(...)` lines in the constructor 1:1. All four

|---|---|---|---|
| `arches` | [1, 6] (full) | candidate | any count tiles seamlessly |
| `tracery` | [0.3, 0.9] | candidate | extremes excluded: 0 blank windows, 1 saturates |
| `glitch` | [0, 0.5] | candidate | upper capped — full glitch is a takeover, not a jump |

## Simulation-principles compliance

- **Growth (sustained motion)** — the nave rises 45 rows over a full 0→1 raise.
  The eased `growthShown` is rate-capped to `GROWTH_SECONDS = lin(energy, 8, 5)`:
  **5.0 s at e=1, exactly at the ≥5 s cap**, 8 s at e=0. Even if the arrange
  automation cuts Growth instantly, the on-sculpture raise honors the cap.
- **CA tracery flow (sustained motion)** — one row per step; free-run step
  interval 1.2 s (peak) → 3.5 s (ambient), so a full-height (45-row) traversal
  is ≥54 s. Well above the cap. Sync-on steps land on the grid but are still one
  row per crossing.
- **Bend glitch (event-like)** — ~2.2 s decay, ≥1.5 s event minimum; the shear
  is a transient distortion, not a traversal.
- **Heal glow (event-like)** — ~3 s settle, ≥1.5 s; no spatial traversal.
- **TearDown (event-like)** — ~3.5 s rise + ~0.9 s hold + ~3.5 s fall; each leg
  ≥1.5 s. A staggered fold, not a continuous sweep across the whole sculpture at
  a single rate.
- **Starfield** — slow twinkle (period ~10 s) + very slow parallax drift; no
  fast motion.
- **Bold forms / contrast** — hard-edged stone ribs/piers/mullions with a dim
  window field and posterized (binary) CA tracery; no fine gradients. Depth of
  field comes from the growth reveal, not texture. CURATE: verify the tracery
  (binary CA at LED pitch) reads as intentional generated tracery, not noise —
  raise `RIB_ROWS`/lower `Tracery` if it shimmers.

## CURATE notes

Every value below is a first-pass guess to be tuned on hardware
(2026-07-07, Claude autonomous session). Grep `CURATE:` in the `.java`.

- Arch geometry: `SPRING_FRAC` 0.50, `APEX_FRAC` 0.92, `ARCH_EXP` 0.6 (pointed
  vs. round), `RIB_ROWS` 2, `BASE_ROWS` 2, `PIER_NORM` 0.82, `MULLION_HALF`
  0.03 — the whole silhouette is unverified; confirm it reads as a gothic
  lancet arch, not a tent, at LED resolution.
- `BUTTRESS_ONSET` 0.60 and the strut reach/drop (6/8 columns) — the flying
  buttresses are a rough diagonal-strut stand-in; confirm they read as
  buttresses and don't clutter. They are drawn for the cube; the cylinder call
  site skips them.
- Color: fallback cool stone (H255/S55/B62) and gold (H45/S85/B100) picked
  blind; `WINDOW_BACK` 0.10, `TRACERY_BOOST` 1.7, `TRACERY_HUE_OFFSET` 20°. The
  cool→gold hue lerp deliberately takes the shortest wheel path (through
  magenta/red) — confirm that reads as "occult," not muddy. Palette cache uses
  swatch[0] as cool and swatch[last] as gold.
- Glitch: `GLITCH_HUE_DEG` 90, `MAX_SHEAR` 6 columns, `shearNoise` a cheap
  sin-hash — confirm the bend reads as dissonance.
- Teardown: `TEAR_SLICES` 8, stagger constants in `warpCopy`, rise/hold/fall
  3.5/0.9/3.5 s — the page-fold is an interpretation of "rotate slices 90° as a
  codex"; confirm the read.
- Starfield: `STAR_ONSET` 0.15, `NUM_STARS` 14, `STAR_MAX_B` 55, hues 0-14
  (reds), slow speeds/drift — confirm the valley holds as an uncomfortable
  near-dark field, not a busy starscape.
- CA: default rule 90 (Sierpinski); downward flow direction and seed (single
  center cell, or ~25% random on `Seed`) unverified for the intended "generated
  tracery" look. `Rule` is a knob but not a jump candidate (many rules are dull
  or blank).
- Energy endpoints (growth 8→5 s, CA 3.5→1.2 s) unverified against the track.
