## Recommended approach

**PRIMARY — Instagram DM @quackhouse.** They personally run it, they own the
master and wrote the song, and the ask (free, non-commercial, art-context, nothing
sold, credit them prominently) is exactly the low-stakes request artists in the
rave/Burning-Man scene tend to say yes to. Keep it short: what the piece is, that
it's non-commercial/free at Burning Man, that no recording is sold, that you'll
credit them, and a one-line "is it OK to use 'Bliss' as the soundtrack?"

**FALLBACK — X/Twitter DM @quackhouse**, then a **SoundCloud message** (soundcloud.com/ducky),
then **Bluesky**. If DMs go unanswered after a couple of weeks, a public reply/mention
on a recent post (politely asking them to check DMs) is a reasonable nudge.

**Why this is the highest-yes path:** the "~half say yes" odds improve sharply
when (a) there's no label/manager filter between you and the decision-maker, and
(b) the decision-maker is a single artist who controls all the rights. Both are
true here. Contacting Secret Songs or any of their past labels would be slower and
route around the actual rights holder.
