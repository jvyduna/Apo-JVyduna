## 2. Longer bio paragraph (optional email expansion)

Jeff Vyduna is a San Francisco–based creative technologist and light artist. He
served as **lighting lead / designer for *Titanic's End***, a Burning Man art car
that debuted in 2022 and drives its shows with an open-source software lighting
stack — reportedly ~85,000 LEDs run from LX Studio (the same Chromatik/LX
framework), plus about a dozen Kvant lasers ([titanicsend.com](https://www.titanicsend.com/),
[jeffvyduna.com](https://www.jeffvyduna.com/)). He is part of the **core team
around Pixelblaze**, Ben Hencke's widely used WiFi LED pattern platform, and has
authored and shared a large body of open patterns and example code — including a
music sequencer that other creators have built on, Perlin-noise and 3D-rotation
effects, and a WiFi music-sync project ([jeff.pb.gallery](https://jeff.pb.gallery/),
[github.com/jvyduna/pb-examples](https://github.com/jvyduna/pb-examples)). His
open-source light work also includes PangoScripts for laser programming in
Pangolin's Beyond software ([github.com/jvyduna/PaingoScripts](https://github.com/jvyduna/PaingoScripts))
and the animated **BCWW sign** project for Burning Man
([github.com/jvyduna/BCWW-sign-2018](https://github.com/jvyduna/BCWW-sign-2018)).
He is a trained, ILDA-certified **Laser Safety Officer**
([ilda.com/LSOgraduates.html](https://www.ilda.com/LSOgraduates.html)) and has
contributed laser design to fine-art laser cinema such as Chris Benchetler's
*Mountains of the Moon* using Kvant lasers and Beyond
([pangolin.com](https://de.pangolin.com/blogs/news/mountains-of-the-moon)). He is
currently composing native light patterns for **Apotheneum**, a building-scale
immersive LED installation (a ~30-ft illuminated cylinder within a 40-ft cube,
~26,000 LEDs) created by architect Anthony Fieldman with a team led by Mark Slee
([anthonyfieldman.com](http://www.anthonyfieldman.com/architecture/apotheneum-ca9sp)).
Outside of light art, Jeff is a **co-founder (and former CEO) of Poll
Everywhere**, the live-audience response platform (Y Combinator, 2008); he holds
an MBA from MIT Sloan and a BS in Electrical Engineering / Computer Science from
the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign
([en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poll_Everywhere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poll_Everywhere),
[worldatwork.org](https://worldatwork.org/people/jeff-vyduna)).
