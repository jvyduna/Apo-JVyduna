# Permission request research — "Rafters" by Lusine

**Use case:** Soundtrack to a NON-COMMERCIAL light-art performance at Burning Man
(free, art context, no recording sold, no tickets sold for it). Goal: the single
lowest-friction contact most likely to be read and to say yes.

**Research date:** 2026-07-07. Do NOT contact anyone — this is research only.

---

## TL;DR recommendation

- **PRIMARY:** Email the artist directly at **`Jeff@lusineweb.com`** — the
  "General Inquiries" address published on Lusine's own official site. This is
  Jeff McIlwain's personal inbox, not a label/agency gatekeeper. For an indie,
  self-directed artist on a small tastemaker label, a short friendly note to the
  artist himself is the highest-probability "read + yes" path for a low-stakes,
  non-commercial art use. **Confidence: High** that the address is real and
  monitored; **Med** that it yields a yes (per the ~50% reply / ~50% yes prior,
  but art-context/non-commercial framing skews favorable).
- **FALLBACK:** Message via **Bandcamp** (`lusine.bandcamp.com`) — artists get
  and read fan/DM messages there — and/or **Instagram DM** (`@lusine_music`).
  Same person, second channel if the email goes cold after ~1-2 weeks.
- **LABEL PATH (secondary):** Ghostly International (`ghostly.com`) owns/releases
  the master. Useful only if the artist defers to the label; slower and more
  gatekept, so not the first move for a non-commercial art use.

## Reasoning: who actually reads + says yes

- Jeff McIlwain is an indie artist who publishes his **own** email on his **own**
  site and labels it "General Inquiries" — a clear invitation to be contacted
  directly. That inbox is read by the decision-maker himself, with no agency or
  label gatekeeper filtering a non-commercial art request.
- The ask is low-stakes and flattering (a light artist wants to choreograph a
  free Burning Man art piece to his track, nothing sold, no recording
  distributed). That framing is exactly the kind an artist replies "yes, cool,
  go for it" to. Keep the note short, specific, non-commercial, and offer credit.
- The label (Ghostly) is a reasonable *fallback/escalation* if the artist wants
  formal sign-off, but leading with the label adds friction and a gatekeeper for
  no benefit at this stage.

## Suggested first message shape (not sent)

Short email to `Jeff@lusineweb.com`: who Jeff Vyduna is, that it's a free
non-commercial light-art performance at Burning Man, that "Rafters" from Long
Light would be the soundtrack, that nothing is sold and no recording is
distributed, offer of credit, and a simple "is that okay with you?" Ask for any
attribution wording he'd like.
