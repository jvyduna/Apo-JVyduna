## TL;DR recommendation

- **PRIMARY: message the artist directly via his official Bandcamp** (the R Plus Seven page
  has a live "Contact Oneohtrix Point Never" link → emails his team). This is the single
  lowest-friction, most-likely-to-be-read channel for a low-stakes art blessing. **Confidence: High**
  that it reaches his camp; Med that it gets a yes (but an experimental artist + Burning Man
  light-art framing is a favorable match).
- **FALLBACK 1: Instagram DM `@eccopn` and/or Twitter/X `@0PN`** — same person's socials,
  also direct-to-camp.
- **FALLBACK 2 (formal, only if he wants a paper license): Warp licensing** `licensing@warp.net`
  / warplicensing.net request form. Note: **Warp explicitly does NOT grant fee-free licences**
  (their FAQ), so this channel is fee-oriented and higher-friction — worse odds for a free art use.

**Why artist-direct beats the label here:** Warp is a real indie with a formal, fee-charging
licensing desk that will treat this as a sync/grand-rights request and likely quote money or
ignore a tiny non-commercial ask. Lopatin himself is the person most likely to simply say
"yeah, cool" to a free art use. Frame it as: free, non-commercial, no recording distributed,
Apotheneum LED art / Burning Man, playback only, happy to credit.

> Practical note: playing a *recording* at a free public art event is normally covered by the
> event's own public-performance (PRO blanket) situation, not something you individually clear
> with the label. So this is really a **courtesy/goodwill "blessing"** ask, not a legal
> requirement — which further argues for the friendly artist-direct route over the licensing desk.
