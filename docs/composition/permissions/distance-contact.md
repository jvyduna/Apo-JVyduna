# Permission research: "Our Distance" — Helios (Keith Kenniff)

Goal: request permission to use the Helios recording as the soundtrack to a
**non-commercial** light-art performance at Burning Man (free, art-context, no
recording sold, no tickets sold for the piece). Target the single lowest-friction
contact most likely to be read and to say yes.

## Which track (two candidates — both same artist)

- **"Our Distance"** — track 3 on the album **Domicile** (2020). This is the
  literal title match. Bandcamp lists it under *Helios (Keith Kenniff + Hollie
  Kenniff)*, "all rights reserved."
  Source: https://heliosmusic.bandcamp.com/track/our-distance (High)
- **"Our Distance and That of Trees"** — opening track of **Eingya** (2006, Type
  Records). This is the famous, oft-licensed Helios piece; the seed flags it as
  the likely intended track.
  Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eingya (High)

Both are Keith Kenniff writing/performing as Helios, so the contact path is the
**same regardless of which track**. Confirm the exact track with Jeff, but it
does not change who to email.

## Key finding: the artist self-administers everything

Keith + Hollie Kenniff run **Unseen** (unseen-music.com), their own boutique
licensing/composing company (est. 2010). Kenniff self-releases as Helios and
handles his own sync licensing directly. This is the ideal indie case: **the
songwriter, the recording artist, and the licensing contact are all one reachable
person, with a published email.** No gatekeeper label or publisher stands between
you and a yes.

## Rights-holder breakdown

- **Master recording owner / label**
  - *Domicile* (2020): self-released; Bandcamp shows "all rights reserved" under
    the artist, no label imprint — i.e. Kenniff controls the master directly.
    (Med-High) https://heliosmusic.bandcamp.com/album/domicile
  - *Eingya* (2006): originally **Type Records** (UK, John Twells / Stefan
    Lewandowski). Type is long defunct; rights have effectively reverted to /
    are handled by Kenniff, who now self-administers his catalog via Unseen.
    (Med) https://www.discogs.com/master/26602-Helios-Eingya
  - Either way, the artist is reachable directly — this is an indie/self-managed
    situation, not a major-label one. (High)

- **Publisher / songwriter / PRO**
  - Songwriter: **Keith Kenniff** (sole writer on Helios material). (High)
  - Publishing: self-published, administered through **Unseen**. No third-party
    publisher gatekeeper found. (Med)
  - PRO: not definitively confirmed from public sources (likely ASCAP or BMI,
    typical for US composers). Not needed for a direct permission ask — Kenniff
    can grant use himself. (Low on the specific PRO)

- **Management / booking**
  - No exclusive personal manager found; Kenniff self-manages via Unseen.
  - A booking listing exists at **AAE Music** (a third-party speakers/talent
    booking aggregator) — this is a paid, commercial live-booking funnel and is
    the **wrong** channel for a low-stakes permission ask. Avoid it.
    https://www.aaemusic.com/artist/keith-kenniff/ (Med)

## DIRECT artist contact paths (best for this use)

1. **Bandcamp message** — the artist page has a "Contact Keith Kenniff + Hollie
   Kenniff" form; artists reliably receive and read these, and the framing
   (a fan/artist writing about their music) fits a low-stakes art ask.
   https://heliosmusic.bandcamp.com/track/our-distance (High)
2. **Direct email — keithkenniff@gmail.com** — published on Unseen's licensing
   page as the "Inquiries" contact ("get in touch about your project"). This is

   https://www.unseen-music.com/license/ (High)
3. **Unseen licensing site / contact** — unseen-music.com (also lists an

4. **Instagram DM** — @kenniffs (personal) and @heliosmusic-adjacent handles;

   https://www.instagram.com/kenniffs/

## Recommendation

**PRIMARY: Email keithkenniff@gmail.com directly (Confidence: High).**
It is his own published inbox, explicitly invited for project inquiries, and
reaches the one person who owns the master, wrote the song, and grants his own
sync — no gatekeeper. Keep it short and warm: who you are, that it's a **free,
non-commercial art piece** at Burning Man, no recording made/sold, no tickets
sold, credit given, and that you'd love his blessing to use "Our Distance" as the
soundtrack to an LED light-art installation. Naming the specific track and showing
genuine appreciation for the music maximizes the "yes."

**FALLBACK: Bandcamp message form** (Confidence: High as a channel). Same person,
but the message lands in a fan/artist context he checks; use it if the email goes
unanswered after ~1-2 weeks, or send both. Instagram DM (@kenniffs) is a light
third nudge.

**Avoid:** AAE Music booking (commercial live-booking aggregator) and any generic
sync-library flow — both add friction and a commercial frame that doesn't match a
free art use.

Reasoning: with ~half of rights holders never replying, the win here is that
Kenniff is a self-managed indie who *publishes his own email and invites contact*.
A single personal, appreciative email to keithkenniff@gmail.com is both the
most-likely-read and the most-likely-yes path.
