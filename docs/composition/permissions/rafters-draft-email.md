**Subject:** Permission to set "Rafters" to light for a non-commercial art piece?

"Rafters" has been on repeat for me - that slow, weightless build and the way the

I'm Jeff Vyduna, a light artist who builds and performs LED and laser installations. I've recently worked on [a film](https://www.instagram.com/p/DTQroIQDx6p/?img_index=1) for the Grateful Dead that won an industry award, designed for [Disco Lines](https://youtu.be/USZJBekMrFI?t=155), built [art](https://www.instagram.com/p/CxhbsE3u18b/?hl=en) [cars](https://www.instagram.com/p/DNW8YERR-2f/) with >100K LEDs, and done interactive lighting for the [Vacaville Children's Library](https://www.instagram.com/p/DSOUM1XgXNA/?hl=en). I'm now doing volunteer work to compose a short, carefully choreographed non-commercial piece for [*Apotheneum*](https://youtu.be/v6qvJyfvgyg?t=186), a monumental LED installation, and I'd love your blessing to set one of your songs to light for a small performance at Burning Man.

The ask is small: your okay to sync the recording of "Rafters" to an LED light
show at Burning Man - free, non-commercial, no tickets, no recording sold or
distributed - for roughly 3-5 minutes of the piece. You'd get full credit however
you'd like it worded, and I'm glad to send a private preview so you can see what
your music becomes in light. If it's not for you, no worries at all - I'm greatful
either way.

Thank you for the music,
Jeff Vyduna
