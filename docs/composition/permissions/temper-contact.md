# Permission research — "Temper The Wound" (Kalia Vandever)

Goal: request permission to use the master recording as soundtrack to a **free,
non-commercial light-art performance at Burning Man** (art context, no recording
sold, no tickets sold for it). Priority = the single lowest-friction contact most
likely to actually be read and to say yes for a low-stakes art use.

Researched 2026-07-07. Do NOT contact anyone yet — this is research only.

## Recommendation

**PRIMARY — Management email: hello@hannahhouser.com (Hannah Houser / HH
Creative).** Confidence **High** it will be *read*; **Med-High** it says yes.
Reasoning: it's a monitored, professional inbox whose remit is literally
"Management & **Commissions**" — i.e., the art/creative-use lane, not the cold
corporate-sync lane. A short, warm note framing a *free, non-commercial,
no-recording-sold* Burning Man light-art use is exactly the low-stakes,
good-story ask a solo artist's manager forwards to the artist with a thumbs-up.
Going through management (vs. the label's press-only inbox) also means one email
covers both master and the artist-as-sole-songwriter, since she controls both.

**FALLBACK 1 — Direct to the artist, Instagram DM @kalia.vandever.** Confidence
Med-High to be read. A personal, heartfelt DM about using her ambient/healing
trombone piece in a light-art installation is the kind of message artists in this
scene respond warmly to; bypasses gatekeepers entirely. Pair with a Bandcamp
message for redundancy. Best if management goes quiet after ~1–2 weeks.

**FALLBACK 2 — Sync/licensing: licensing@intlanthem.com (Scottie McNiece).**
The "correct" formal channel, but higher-friction: a licensing desk may default
to paperwork/fees even for a free art use, which can slow or over-formalize a
low-stakes request. Use only if management/artist route stalls or if a written
license is explicitly wanted.

**Why not the label first:** AKP publishes only a *press* address and a generic
web form — lower odds of being read by a decision-maker than the artist's own
management, and it doesn't cover the songwriter side (she does).

**Framing tips (any channel):** lead with the art, not the legal ask — it's
free, non-commercial, no recording sold, no tickets; the piece's ambient/healing
character fits a contemplative light installation; you're seeking her blessing/
permission (offer credit + a link to the finished piece). This maximizes the
"~half say yes" odds.
