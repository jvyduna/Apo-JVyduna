# Composition Brief — "Rafters" (Lusine)

> Song 3 of 6 in *Communicating* (Jeff Vyduna, Apotheneum, ~21 min). Machine-drafted
> brief synthesizing MIR structure + three research lenses. Read alongside
> `_SOURCE-composition-brief.md` and `rafters.mir.json`.

## 1. Snapshot

- **Artist / title:** Lusine (Jeff McIlwain, Seattle) — "Rafters," track 10 of
  *Long Light* (Ghostly International, GI-427, Sept 15 2023). **Note:** the setlist
  seed's *Sensorimotor* attribution is wrong — all three research passes independently
  corrected this. Artist and label (Ghostly) are right.
- **Duration:** 2:58 (178.7 s, MIR).
- **Key:** G minor (high confidence — keyfinder + librosa agree).
- **Tempo / grid:** rigid, MIR-analyzed **140 BPM** (`beat_this` 142.9), 4/4, 16th
  subdivision, 105 bars. The setlist sheet's "70 BPM" is the **perceived half-time
  pulse** — the kick lands on the half-time backbone while the grid counts 140. Lock
  droplet/event motion to the 70 feel; reserve 16ths for moiré/flicker detail.
- **Arc placement & emotional beat:** the **"traveling away"** chapter. Songs 1–2
  (childbirth/healing, then bright childhood screensaver-tech play) sit in the nest;
  Rafters is the **first departure and first doubt** — leaving the loft. It "seems
  young, turns brooding, ends lush" (Jeff), which maps cleanly onto McIlwain's stated
  album theme of contemporary anxiety and "a fun house mirror situation" where "you
  don't know what is real." The word *rafters* itself — overhead beams of a house or
  church loft — plus the track's signature **looped train-track sample** ("fluted
  urgency," HearFeel) gives the exact emotional image: a young soul looking up at the
  architecture it was raised inside, then boarding a train out of it. This is where the
  personal religion first becomes something to travel *away from*.

**Shape in one line:** long bare-Gm adolescence (0:00–1:22) → a single lush harmonic
bloom at the maj7 (1:22–2:20, peak 2:03) → strip back to bare Gm with the nervous
fluted lead exposed (2:17–2:44) → hollow silence (2:44–2:58). The lush is a *middle*
event; the actual ending is empty and unresolved — perfect for "traveling away into
uncertainty."

## 3. Reception & meaning

Confidence caveat up front: **track-specific commentary is genuinely thin** — Rafters
is a deep-cut instrumental interlude, so all three lenses lean on album-level material.
Treat the emotional palette as solid at album level, plausible-but-lightly-sourced at
the single-track level.

- **How listeners feel it (reactions):** contemplative, introspective, slightly uneasy,
  dreamlike; the reflective counterweight to *Long Light*'s brighter microhouse/techno
  cuts. The one recurring sonic landmark listeners/critics project is **movement/transit
  — a "looped train-track sample" with "fluted urgency"**
  ([HearFeel](https://hearfeel.co.uk/2023/11/07/lusine-long-light/)). Album-level fan
  warmth: *"Fine electronic music with meaning and integrity"*, *"a wonderful
  accompaniment to being alive"*, loops that "take you somewhere, pleasurably"
  ([Bandcamp](https://lusine.bandcamp.com/album/long-light)); reviewers describe Lusine's
  loops as "generating dreamscapes" that "induce an overall peace in your mind"
  ([HHV](https://www.hhv-mag.com/review/lusine-long-light/?lang=en)). No crowd-cited
  timestamp exists.
- **Artist's stated vision (album-level; no track statement exists):** McIlwain frames
  *Long Light* around contemporary anxiety and unstable perception — *"a sort of paranoia
  where you don't know what is real… an age of high anxiety… a fun house mirror
  situation."* The album title is from Benoît Pioulard's lyric *"long light signalling
  the fall again"* — seasonal turn / fading light. His process: build each track from a
  **single core element outward**, "dynamic yet minimalist," and *"music making is a
  struggle and you have to have a ton of patience"*
  ([Inverted Audio](https://inverted-audio.com/lusine-lines-long-light-ghostly-international/),
  [XLR8R](https://xlr8r.com/news/lusine-is-back-with-a-new-album/)). Rafters is a
  wordless instrumental — no lyric intent to draw on.
- **Critics' take:** grouped with "Faceless" and "Plateau" as the album's
  **ambient-minded, contemplative core** — "Lusine's strongest instrumental expressions,"
  the introspective counterweight. HearFeel's is the single strongest track-level phrase:
  *"the penultimate 'Rafters' in its fluted urgency,"* part of a **"nervous and
  self-conscious core, fearful of stagnation,"** on a record praised for "clarity and
  refinement" ([HearFeel](https://hearfeel.co.uk/2023/11/07/lusine-long-light/),
  [Bandcamp/Ghostly blurb](https://lusine.bandcamp.com/album/long-light)). Coverage is
  modest — no Pitchfork/RA/Quietus review; AOTY aggregate ~70
  ([AOTY](https://www.albumoftheyear.org/album/727821-lusine-long-light.php)).

**The load-bearing phrase for the visuals:** *"nervous and self-conscious core, fearful
of stagnation."* Fear of stagnation is, almost literally, the anxiety of cellular life
(patterns that must keep moving or die) and of a train that can't stop — that's the
design compass.

## 4. Jeff's existing ideas (from the setlist sheet)

Verbatim column: *"Seems young, turns brooding, ends lush."* Four idea seeds:

1. **Blinkfade / life / cellular automata (CA)** — fast-paced imagery, Kendrick-Lamar-video
   aesthetic (rapid-cut, kinetic).
2. **Window rain** — pseudo-religious symbols appear and morph on the glass.
3. **Stop-motion of mirror shadows** — placed on the "(1)e&a" of the beat (specific
   syncopated 16th placement).
4. **Moiré on 1/16 notes** — interference shimmer driven by the 16th grid.

Grouped: (1) and (3) are *kinetic/generative-life* ideas; (2) and (4) are
*surface/glass/interference* ideas. Both families read as "young curiosity that curdles
into unease" — exactly the arc.

## 5. Artistic feedback & direction

**Make it the anti-*bliss*.** Song 2 is bright neon screensaver-tech (Mountains,
Mystify, Outrun, Pipes3D, Rubik) — hard-edged, saturated, playful. Rafters must feel
**organic, wet, monochrome-leaning, and anxious** by contrast. **Deliberately avoid the
screensaver vocabulary here** (don't reuse Outrun/Pipes3D/Rubik/Satori/Mountains as
themselves — that's *bliss*'s identity, and reusing it flattens the arc). Pull the palette
down into **cold G-minor blues/greys** for most of the run, and spend saturation only
**once**, as a warm bloom, on the maj7.

**Palette:** cold spine — desaturated slate/steel blues with near-black negative space
(Gareth `#33CCFF/#CC99FF/#FFB38A` read cold, or a custom cool-grey triad). Reserve the
warm third color (`#FFB38A` / a cream-amber) exclusively for the **1:22–2:20 maj7 bloom**
so the lush section is the only time the room goes warm. Return to cold + then to near-black
for the outro.

**Choreography anchors (the timeline is the storyboard):**
- **0:00–1:22 — "young":** sparse, single-element, low fill. Honor McIlwain's "build from
  one core element outward" — start with *one* live thing (one glider, one droplet, one
  glyph) and let it multiply. Cold, quiet, exposed.
- **1:22 (bar 51) — the turn:** the harmonic move to Ebmaj7 is the single most important
  cue in the track. Warm the palette *here*, add depth/trails, let the field open up.
- **1:56–2:20 — "lush" plateau (peak ~2:03, Bb6 at ~2:10):** fullest, warmest, most
  dreamlike. This is the emotional apex — commit to it. Use feedback/trails to smear it
  into the "dreamscape" reviewers describe.
- **2:16–2:22 — the seam:** the vocal-chop burst is a built-in hard cut / stutter moment —
  a Kendrick-style rapid-cut or a Rip-style negative-space tear right on the cluster.
- **2:17–2:44 — brooding coda:** yank the warmth back out, drop to bare Gm, and **bring the
  fluted/train lead forward** (the "other" stem literally does this — centroid jumps
  brighter). Nervous, exposed, restless. This is "fearful of stagnation" — keep motion
  going but strip the comfort.
- **2:44–2:58 — hollow outro:** let it die. Near-black, a few last ghost droplets/glyphs
  fading (mirror the 2:48–2:53 vocal ghosts), then dark. Do **not** resolve — the departure
  is unresolved on purpose; that sets up song 4 (opus) and the "accepting" chapter later.

**Lean into:** verticality (rafters = overhead beams; rain falls; the train passes) — use
the cube's **height** and the cylinder's ring, motion mostly on the **vertical axis**.
Single-element-outward generativity. One warm bloom. A hollow ending.
**Avoid:** neon saturation as a default, busy multi-pattern collage (this track is
minimalist — one strong idea beats five), and any triumphant resolution.

**How it differs from neighbors:** warmer/brighter *bliss* before it and the deadpan
*opus* troll after it bracket a track that should be the piece's first **melancholy,
searching** moment — the emotional dip that makes the later "reconstruction" land.

## 6. Pattern & effect plan

Ranked. New classes live under `patterns/rafters/` (mirroring `patterns/bliss/`);
all follow the conventions (palette-driven, `Audio` depth knob defaulting to 0 =
screensaver, `TempoLock` grid sync, ≥3 non-meta triggers, zero-alloc render, ≥5 s
full-traversal cap).

1. **★ HERO — `GlassRain` (NEW pattern)** — rain-on-a-loft-window: droplets spawn and
   streak **down** the cube faces + cylinder, leaving wet vertical trails, with occasional
   **religious symbols** (cross, eye, sun/halo, fish) condensing out of the rain and
   morphing/dissolving. Directly realizes Jeff's Idea 2 (window rain + morphing symbols)
   and carries the *entire* arc with one instrument: sparse cold drops (0:00–1:22) →
   warm bloom + heavier, trail-smeared rain at the maj7 (1:22–2:20) → cold nervous
   streaks with the fluted lead (2:17–2:44) → a few last drops fading to black
   (2:44–2:58). **TempoLock** droplet spawn to the 70 half-time pulse (16ths for
   fine spatter). Triggers: (a) heavy-drop burst on kick, (b) symbol-condense on the
   2:16 vocal cluster, (c) sheet-wash on section change. Palette-driven; `Audio` knob
   scales spawn density/streak length. Verticality suits the geometry perfectly.
   Symbols can reuse **`PixelFont5`/Text** scaffolding for bitmap glyphs (author a small
   religious-symbol glyph set alongside the alphabet). Best first-pass ROI: readable on
   LEDs, single strong idea, spans all 2:58.

2. **`Life` / cellular automata (NEW pattern)** — Conway-style CA on the cube faces
   (and interior), seeded from a single cell and grown outward. Realizes Idea 1 and *is*
   the "fearful of stagnation" thesis (CA colonies must keep moving or die). "Seems young"
   = simple glider play in the intro; "turns brooding" = decay/overpopulation collapse.
   Strong #2 hero if GlassRain proves too literal; risk is LED legibility (CA can look
   like noise — mitigate with large cells, slow generations locked to the beat, trails).
   Triggers: reseed on downbeat, inject-glider on snare, "rapture"/clear on section change.

3. **Moiré (reuse `Mystify`, repurposed)** — Idea 4. Mystify already draws moving
   polygon line-webs; tighten line spacing and run two counter-phased layers so overlap
   produces **moiré interference that shimmers on the 16th grid**. Cold palette, low
   brightness — a nervous background texture, ideal under the 2:17–2:44 fluted coda.
   Reuses an existing class with parameter changes, not a rewrite.

4. **`BlitFeedback` (reuse effect) — the "fun house mirror"** — frame-buffer feedback
   layered over the hero for the **1:22–2:20 lush plateau** to smear it into the
   "dreamscape" reviewers describe, and again as a **destabilizing warp** in the brooding
   coda (unstable perception = McIlwain's stated theme). Automate feedback amount up at
   1:22, peak at 2:03, back to zero by 2:44. Direct reuse.

5. **Nervous ridgelines (reuse `UnknownPleasures`)** — stacked horizontal waveform
   ridgelines driven by the mix/`other` envelope to render the **train-track loop's
   "fluted urgency"** as restless scan-lines. Fits the coda (2:17–2:44) where the lead
   surfaces. Reuse as-is with an envelope source swap; good B-roll / secondary channel.

6. **`Rip` effect (reuse) at the 2:16 seam** — the dense vocal-chop cluster at the
   full→beats break is a natural negative-space tear (FFT/trigger driven). One decisive
   Rip on that cluster gives the Kendrick-style hard cut from Idea 1 without a whole
   rapid-cut pattern. Direct reuse.

7. **`Text` / symbol morph (reuse) — optional** — if the morphing religious symbols in
   the hero want their own dedicated channel/moment rather than living inside GlassRain,
   Text + a religious-symbol glyph set renders and cross-fades them (e.g. a single symbol
   forming at the 1:22 turn). Reuse; overlaps with hero item 1's glyph work.

**Not for this song:** Outrun, Pipes3D, Rubik, Satori, Mountains, Lorre, Terraform, Zot —
keep the *bliss* screensaver set and Zot's lightning out of Rafters to protect the arc's
tonal contrast.
