## 1. Snapshot

- **Artist / title:** Helios (Keith Kenniff, with Hollie Kenniff) — "Our Distance,"
  track 3 on *Domicile* (Ghostly International, 11 Dec 2020). NOTE: the setlist's
  "Our Distance and That of Trees" is a phantom title — the real track is just
  "Our Distance." All three research lenses independently confirm this.
- **Duration:** 5:24 (323.6 s) — the longest piece in the set, and the finale.
- **Key:** C major (global, high agreement — keyfinder + librosa concur). Harmony
  is static and pedal-toned: **C · F · Fm · C/G · Fmaj7 · F6** (118 of 223 bars sit
  on C). The emotional signature is the **F → Fm** shading — a borrowed minor iv
  under a tonic pedal, the "plagal ache." Plagal motion (IV–I, the *Amen* cadence)
  is literally the sound of a church resolution.
- **Tempo / grid:** `tracked` ambient, referenceBpm 120, 4/4, 16th subdivision —
  but the tracker drops out repeatedly (real gaps at ~1:29, ~1:48, ~2:38→2:54,
  ~3:58, ~4:11, ~4:55). Treat it as **breathing ambient, not a metronome**: lock
  slow gestures to the ~1.4 s bar/pad swell, let downbeats *nudge* rather than drive.
- **Arc position — the reconstruction.** After the dissonance-and-resolution of
  *Chrome Country* (accepting the father's religion), *Our Distance* is where the
  protagonist **rebuilds a personal faith** — warm, self-authored, at home.
  Kenniff literally wrote *Domicile* as "a safe space to go to without judgement
  or expectation." That is the emotional beat: sanctuary, wholeness, a religion
  reassembled from scattered parts into something that breathes. It should land as
  arrival and rest — the exhale the whole 21 minutes has been walking toward.

## 3. Reception & meaning

Three lenses, all agreeing the track itself is under-documented; meaning must be
read at the **album level** (*Domicile*). Confidence noted per claim.

- **How listeners feel it (solid on the cluster, thin per-track).** Consistent
  vocabulary across crowd tags and fan comments: *calm, warm, soothing, floating,
  spacious, boundless, "a balm."* Last.fm tags the track **ambient / downtempo /
  IDM / "ethereal ambient"** (14.9K listeners). Fans single out **the strings** as
  the emotional pull. No standout-timestamp consensus exists.
  [Last.fm](https://www.last.fm/music/Helios/_/Our+Distance) ·
  [Bandcamp — Domicile](https://heliosmusic.bandcamp.com/album/domicile) ·
  [Bandcamp — track](https://heliosmusic.bandcamp.com/track/our-distance)
- **Artist's stated vision (solid, album-level).** Kenniff: "I wanted to write an
  album that inspired **calmness and reflection**; something specifically designed
  to be listened to **at home**." Released into lockdown as "a **safe space** to go
  to without judgement or expectation, without anxiety or stress," and "if we step
  back for a moment perhaps we will be able to step forward again with more purpose."
  Label framing: each piece meant to "gently **color the day**," lingering "the way
  a **window's light or a tree's shadow moves across the wall**" (Eno's ambient
  lineage — and the only tree/light imagery, which is where the seed title's "trees"
  bled in from).
  [Stationary Travels](https://stationarytravels.wordpress.com/2021/01/16/a-sense-of-place-domicile-by-helios/) ·
  [Ghostly](https://ghostly.com/artists/helios)
- **Critics (thin — no per-track review exists).** *Domicile* got little top-tier
  coverage. Best characterization: "**domestic ambient**," Eno's *Music for
  Airports* "transposed to a domestic context," with "somber organ tones [that]
  swell, undulate, and fan out," "unhurried… unstructured." Aggregate reception is
  mixed-to-warm; the recurring knock is **monotony over 40 minutes**. Sharper Helios
  criticism ("hazy ambience… washed-out electronic processing with clear guitar
  melodies and delicate piano," Pitchfork) is actually for *Eingya* (2006) — use as
  aesthetic backdrop only.
  [Stationary Travels](https://stationarytravels.wordpress.com/2021/01/16/a-sense-of-place-domicile-by-helios/) ·
  [AOTY — Domicile](https://www.albumoftheyear.org/album/291287-helios-domicile.php) ·
  [Sputnik — Eingya](https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/56489/Helios-Eingya/)

**Takeaway for the visual:** listeners want *warmth and sanctuary*; the artist wants
*home, calm, stepping back to step forward*; the one real critical risk is
**monotony**. Our job is to honor the calm while giving the eye slow, evolving reasons
to keep watching across 5+ minutes — the visual has to breathe and morph even where
the harmony doesn't.

## 4. Jeff's existing ideas (from the setlist sheet)

Two seeds, both about a living, morphing interior world:

1. **Pulsing orb + the Bethlehem shader** (ported from Pixelblaze) — a breathing
   central light/star.
2. **Ketamine K-hole moments** — textured worlds of **reds, purples, and greens**
   morphing their topology, like the folding worlds in *Inception* and *Interstellar*.

Loose palette notes on the sheet that fit here: "Twinkle starfield bliss," "2 of 4
walls image." (Distance has no assigned named palette — it's open. See §5.)

## 5. Artistic feedback & direction

**The thesis: this is the exhale and the reassembly.** Everything before it has been
motion (traveling away, dissonance, resolution). *Our Distance* should read as
**arrival into a warm interior sanctuary that slowly reconstitutes into wholeness**,
then dims to rest. Lean into the Apotheneum's unique asset the other songs underuse:
**the cube interior**. Make the inside the *home* — a warm breathing core — and the
four outer walls the *world's light moving across the wall* (Kenniff's own image).
The seed's "2 of 4 walls image" instinct is right; I'd push it to *interior-first*.

**Marry Jeff's two seeds instead of choosing.** The pulsing orb (idea 1) is the
**home/heart**; the morphing K-hole topology (idea 2) is the **world outside the
window**. The orb breathes with the pad; the walls morph slowly through warm
topology. That single decision also solves the critics' **monotony** risk: two
coupled systems at different speeds give the eye evolution without ever getting busy.

**Palette — resist neon.** The K-hole seed says "reds, purples, greens," but this is
the *warm-sanctuary* finale, not a rave. Pull toward **amber/rose/deep-teal**
(candlelight-through-a-window). `Gareth` (`#33CCFF · #CC99FF · #FFB38A` — sky/lilac/
peach) is the closest named palette; I'd warm it further, biasing peach/rose and
keeping the greens desaturated and rare. The plagal **F→Fm** ache is the cue to
briefly **desaturate / cool** the whole field for a beat, then let it bloom back warm.

**Choreography anchors (in MIR time):**
- **0:00–0:57** — start dark and nearly empty; a single dim orb-seed on the interior,
  brushed-percussion twinkles as faint starfield. Honor the 0:30 valley by letting it
  breathe almost to black. Do NOT fill the frame early.
- **0:57 (centroid warms to 720 Hz)** — the pad thickens; warm the palette and let the
  orb take on volume. First real light.
- **1:49 (bassline locks, bass 0.33)** — the world gains weight: the outer-wall
  topology becomes legible and starts its slow morph, driven off the bass envelope.
  This is where "home" gets a floor under it.
- **2:38→2:54 (the long breakdown / tracker dropout)** — pull the walls back to sparse
  starfield; let the orb hang alone. The one place to create real space/absence.
- **2:57 (GLOBAL RMS PEAK — primary anchor)** — full bloom: orb at max radius, walls
  at their most saturated warm topology, everything coherent. This is *the* arrival —
  the religion, reassembled. Land it here, not at the end.
- **3:12–3:20 (lead returns)** — a melodic filament (a brighter contour tracing the
  lead) threads across the walls.
- **4:04 (snare peak — secondary anchor)** — one decisive percussive bloom/ripple
  outward from the orb; the only hard "hit" gesture in the piece, so make it count.
- **4:37–4:45 (break, then treble collapses)** — begin the dimming: drain the shimmer
  and cool contours exactly as the top end drains from the mix. The room is quieting.
- **5:07–5:24 (outro/fade)** — the orb contracts to a single point and fades to black
  on the last G2. End in silence and darkness — the piece, and the whole set, at rest.

**What to avoid:** hard tempo-locked strobing or fast traversal (violates the ≥5 s
sustained-motion cap *and* the mood); high-saturation neon; busy geometry; anything
"screensaver-cute" (save Mystify/Pipes/Rubik energy for *Bliss*). Also avoid peaking
visually at the very end — the MIR apex is at **2:57**; the finale should *recede*,
not climax.

**How it differs from neighbors:** *Bliss* is rigid-grid tech maximalism; *Chrome
Country* is dissonant, surprising, sharp-edged. *Our Distance* must be the opposite of
both — **slow, warm, soft-focus, interior, resolving**. It is the only piece that
should feel like *stillness with internal life*, and the only one that ends the room
in darkness.

## 6. Pattern & effect plan (ranked)

1. **★ HERO — `PulseOrb` (breathing sanctuary core) — NEW class (small).**
   A warm volumetric orb centered in the **cube interior**, radius/brightness
   breathing on a slow LFO phase-locked to the ~1.4 s pad swell (TempoLock in a
   *loose* mode; bass/RMS envelope from `AudioReactive` drives radius via the `Audio`
   depth knob, default gentle). Palette-driven warm core → cooler halo. Directly
   realizes Jeff's "pulsing orb" seed and carries the whole emotional arc (bloom at
   2:57, ripple at 4:04, contract-to-point fade at 5:07). Triggers: pad-swell peak,
   bass onset, snare hit (≥3). Tractable first pass; the piece works with this alone.
   *Why new, not reuse:* no existing pattern is a radial breathing volume; this is a
   short, focused class and the emotional centerpiece.

2. **BlitFeedback effect (K-hole morph) — REUSE.** Layer the existing
   `effects/BlitFeedback` frame-buffer feedback over the wall layer to get the
   Inception/Interstellar "topology folding into itself" quality from idea 2 —
   slow feedback zoom/rotate, warm-biased. This is the single highest-leverage reuse
   for the "morphing worlds" seed and does most of the anti-monotony work for free.
   Keep feedback gain low/slow so it reads as drift, not glitch.

3. **Terraform (morphing warm topology on the 4 walls) — REUSE.** Run Terraform on the
   outer cube faces as the "world's light across the wall," recolored to the warm
   sanctuary palette and slowed well under the traversal cap; morph rate ramps in at
   the 1:49 bassline and peaks at 2:57. Pairs with #2 (Terraform as the source field,
   BlitFeedback as the fold). If #2+#3 together feel busy, Terraform-alone is the
   fallback wall layer.

4. **Starfield twinkle — REUSE `Zot` (dial way down) or a tiny NEW sparkle layer.**
   The sheet's "Twinkle starfield bliss" for the sparse intro and the 2:38 breakdown.
   `Zot` (lightning) can be detuned to soft point-twinkles keyed to brushed
   percussion onsets; if that fights its nature, a ~30-line sparkle pattern is cheap.
   Low priority — atmosphere, not structure.

5. **Lead filament — REUSE `MirPitchGrid` or `Lorre`.** For the 0:48 and 3:12 lead
   regions, a brighter moving contour. `MirPitchGrid` can visualize the melodic
   lead's pitch on a wall; `Lorre` (Lorenz swarm), slowed and warm-tinted, gives an
   organic drifting filament. Optional accent, only during the two lead windows.

6. **`Text` on the outside walls — REUSE, sparingly.** Mirrors the *Temper* bookend
   (HEAL/BIRTH/HOPE): a single quiet word on the *outer* surface at the resolution —
   e.g. **HOME** or **AMEN** (the plagal cadence made literal) — fading in near 2:57
   or during the 4:37 break. Interior stays wordless. Use at most once; risk is
   over-literalizing. Consider cutting if the abstract read is strong enough.

*Effects summary:* BlitFeedback is the workhorse reuse (#2); `Rip` does **not** fit
here (its negative-space rimshot rips belong to *Bliss*'s percussive language, not
this calm). Everything obeys: palette-driven, `Audio` depth knob default gentle,
loose TempoLock, ≥3 triggers on the hero, and all motion kept slow under the ≥5 s
traversal cap.
