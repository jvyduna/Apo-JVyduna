# "Rafters" by Lusine — Listener Reactions

## Track identity (verify note)
The context seed was wrong: **"Rafters" is not on *Sensorimotor* (2017).** It is
track 10 on Lusine's *Long Light* (Ghostly International, released Sept 15,
2023). Lusine = Jeff McIlwain, Seattle; Ghostly is the correct rights path.
Bandcamp tags for the track: electronic, ambient, downtempo, IDM, techno.
Sources: [Bandcamp track page](https://lusine.bandcamp.com/track/rafters),
[RYM album](https://rateyourmusic.com/release/album/lusine/long-light.p/),
[Discogs/Wikipedia lineage confirming Ghostly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sensorimotor_(album)).

## Synthesis
Direct ordinary-listener chatter about "Rafters" specifically is **thin** — it is
a deep-cut, ambient interstitial rather than a single, so most commentary is
album-level or from critics. The recurring read: "Rafters" is one of *Long
Light*'s **contemplative, ambient-minded** moments — introspective, slightly
uneasy, dreamlike — set against the album's brighter, head-nodding
microhouse/techno tracks. The most concrete imagery listeners/critics project is
of **movement and transit** (a looped train-track sample) laced with a nervous,
"fluted urgency."

## Findings (each with source)
