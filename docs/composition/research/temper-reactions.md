# "Temper the Wound" — Kalia Vandever — Listener Reactions

**Track:** "Temper the Wound" (3:44), track 6 of *We Fell In Turn* (AKP Recordings,
March 31, 2023) — the solo debut of Brooklyn trombonist/composer Kalia Vandever
(also plays with Harry Styles, Japanese Breakfast). Not to be confused with her
jazz-quartet work or the International Anthem album *Mana* (2025). Verified:
correct artist, correct track.

## Synthesis
The record is solo trombone + voice + effects/looping — improvised, ambient, and
built on drone. The emotional consensus is remarkably tight: **sadness that
consoles.** Two poles recur — *grief/melancholy* and *floating/dreamlike peace* —
and listeners describe the sadness as welcome, healing, protective rather than
bleak. "Temper the Wound" specifically is the album's flight/dream piece: gentle
whirling melody spiraling upward over a luminous drone. Caveat: deep grassroots
listener chatter (Reddit, RYM, YouTube/last.fm shouts) is genuinely sparse for
this niche release; the most vivid *ordinary-listener* voices are on Bandcamp,
while the richest imagery comes from critics.

## Findings
- **Grief is the dominant emotional word.** Bandcamp fan *jiristepan*: "The
  saddest trombone I've heard in many years. And the melodies... melancholic
  beauty." Fan *progdolphin*: "It takes a special talent to turn the trombone
  into a weapon of profound sadness and grieving." — https://kaliavandever.bandcamp.com/album/we-fell-in-turn
- **Sadness framed as comforting/grateful, not bleak.** Foxy Digitalis (Brad
  Rose): "the soundtrack to a beautiful goodbye, the kind of sadness you're
  grateful to have experienced." — https://kaliavandever.bandcamp.com/album/we-fell-in-turn
- **Dreamlike / enveloping / warm.** KCRW-style framing echoed widely: "dreamlike
  and comforting, a woven shawl of sound"; overall "wispy peace." — https://acloserlisten.com/2023/03/28/kalia-vandever-we-fell-in-turn/
- **"Temper the Wound" = flight/floating imagery.** NPR's Tom Huizenga: trombone
  "interleaves loops with gentle, whirling melodies that circle above a drone of
  luminous air"; "with its warm purr, seems to spiral delicately upward to a
  safer space"; "If you've ever taken flight in your dreams, a track like 'Temper
  the Wound'... could be a fitting soundtrack." — https://www.kalw.org/2023-02-03/kalia-vandever-temper-the-wound
- **Versatility / immersive-vs-background.** Bandcamp listener: "it could be very
  legitimately used as background noise, or it could be a full-blown story that
  takes you down many winding paths of tension and emotion. The only difference
  ... is whatever you wanted this album to be." — https://kaliavandever.bandcamp.com/album/we-fell-in-turn
- **Standout-track signal (data, not prose).** last.fm: album has ~8,700
  listeners; "Temper the Wound" is the **2nd-most-listened track (2,672
  listeners)**, behind only "We Wept in Turn" (5,541) — i.e. one of the two songs
  people actually seek out. Tags: *ambient*, *experimental*. — https://www.last.fm/music/Kalia+Vandever/We+Fell+in+Turn
- **Reference points listeners/critics reach for:** Grouper, William Basinski's
  *The Disintegration Loops*, Jeff Parker, and Pharoah Sanders/Floating Points —
  all cited to convey stretched-out, meditative, loop-based stillness. — https://stereogum.com/2212686/kalia-vandever-temper-the-wound/music

## Confidence
- **Solid:** the two-pole emotional read (grief ↔ comforting dream-float), the
  ambient/drone/looping sonic character, "Temper the Wound" as the flight/dream
  track, and its status as a most-played track (last.fm data).
- **Thin:** track-*specific* ordinary-listener quotes — most Bandcamp comments are
  album-level; no substantive Reddit/RYM threads or accessible YouTube/last.fm
  shouts were found. The most concrete imagery ("luminous air," "flight in
  dreams," "beautiful goodbye") is from critics/reviewers, not lay listeners, so
  treat those as curated rather than crowd consensus.
