# "Temper The Wound" — Kalia Vandever — Critic Impressions

## Synthesis

"Temper The Wound" (2023) is the lead single from Kalia Vandever's solo album
**_We Fell In Turn_** (AKP Recordings, March 31, 2023). Vandever is the correct
artist: a New York–based jazz/experimental **trombonist** (collaborations with
Harry Styles, Japanese Breakfast) whose solo work turns the trombone into
ambient, loop-based drone. Do not confuse with her jazz-quartet records; this is
her experimental solo mode. Critics converge on a shared vocabulary: **ambient,
dreamlike, looping, comforting-yet-sorrowful**. The album is conceptually tied
to the sensation of **falling right before waking** and to Vandever's Hawaiian
childhood and _'aumākua_ (ancestral spirit) traditions. Coverage is real but
mostly ambient/jazz-niche outlets (NPR, Stereogum, A Closer Listen, Brainwashed);
no full Pitchfork/Quietus/RA review of this track was found.

## Findings

- **Genre / core characterization:** "basically ambient music — gorgeous,
  searching ambient music"; press materials invoke Grouper, Jeff Parker, and
  William Basinski's _The Disintegration Loops_ for its "stretched-out,
  meditative quality." Label AKP; album rooted in Vandever's Hawai'i childhood.
  — Stereogum, https://stereogum.com/2212686/kalia-vandever-temper-the-wound/music
- **"Trom-drone" / structure:** NPR's Tom Huizenga coins "trom-drone"; the
  trombone "interleaves loops with gentle, whirling melodies that circle above a
  drone of luminous air." Music comes "from the intangible feeling of waking up
  from vivid dreams, particularly the experience of falling right before waking"
  — it "seems to spiral delicately upward to a safer space." Compared to Pharoah
  Sanders & Floating Points' _Promises_ for reverent stillness.
  — NPR, https://www.npr.org/sections/now-playing/2023/02/03/1153888512/kalia-vandever-temper-the-wound
- **Texture / emotional arc (album):** "a woven shawl of sound," "dreamlike and
  comforting"; despite conceptual darkness it conveys "wispy peace" and emotional
  protection; the whole set (~31 min) resolves toward inner stability.
  — A Closer Listen, https://acloserlisten.com/2023/03/28/kalia-vandever-we-fell-in-turn/
- **"Temper the Wound" specifically:** paired with "Held In" as the album's
  emotional core; the track "balance[s] sadness with strength and survival."
  Overall sound is "fresh, abstract and dreamlike" with "beguiling warmth, subtle
  tension, and comforting understatement"; phrases "loop, fold, or crumble
  slightly into themselves in a barely decipherable but extremely melodic manner."
  Comparisons: Jan Garbarek, Laura Cannell, Kali Malone.
  — Brainwashed, https://brainwashed.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=32716

## Confidence

- **Solid:** artist identity, that it's a solo ambient-trombone track from
  _We Fell In Turn_ (AKP, 2023); the "falling before waking" / dream concept; the
  ambient/drone/loop characterization and "trom-drone" coin (multiple sources).
- **Thin:** no Pitchfork, Quietus, RA, PopMatters, or Tiny Mix Tapes review of
  *this specific track*. AllMusic returned a 403 (unread). The most track-specific
  critical read is Brainwashed's; most other outlets discuss the single as an
  album preview rather than analyzing it in isolation.
