# "Temper The Wound" — Kalia Vandever: Artist's Stated Vision

## Synthesis

"Temper The Wound" is the lead single (track 6) from **We Fell In Turn** (AKP
Recordings, March 31, 2023), the solo debut of trombonist/composer/vocalist
**Kalia Vandever** — a Brooklyn/NY-based Hawaiian-American jazz artist (leads a
quartet; not to be confused with any namesake). The album is improvised solo
trombone + voice + effects pedals (looping/delay), recorded over three days in
upstate New York. The whole record processes childhood memory, love, and loss,
threaded through the Hawaiian concept of ʻaumākua — ancestral spirit guides that
appear in dreams. Track-specific: Vandever says "Temper The Wound" is about her
**parents' separation at the end of middle school**, and about *how you choose to
move through pain* rather than the pain itself — a healing/processing arc that
the music enacts by spiraling gently *upward* into safety despite its
falling-dream origin. Sonically it is "trom-drone": looped trombone melodies
circling above a luminous drone, warm and reverent.

## Findings (each with source)

- Album draws on childhood memories and her "maternal homeland of Hawaii";
  improvised solo trombone/voice/effects; recorded over three days upstate;
  producer Lee Meadvin gave "prompts before I started improvising." Release: AKP
  Recordings, 2023-03-31. — https://kaliavandever.bandcamp.com/album/we-fell-in-turn
- Her own framing of the track: it addresses her parents' separation at the end
  of middle school; "It's how you decide to move through that pain." She began
  using effects pedals in pandemic lockdown to loop/delay and "layer her
  melodies," giving the trombone a "gauzy, gossamer quality." —
  https://daily.bandcamp.com/features/kalia-vandever-we-fell-in-turn-interview
- Dream imagery is the album's engine: inspiration "from the intangible feeling
  of waking up from vivid dreams, particularly the experience of falling right
  before waking" (source of the title *We Fell In Turn*). Hawaiian ʻaumākua /
  spirit guides visit her dreams and surface childhood memories. Critic Tom
  Huizenga calls it "trom-drone." —
  https://www.kalw.org/2023-02-03/kalia-vandever-temper-the-wound (NPR-syndicated)
- Memories of her grandfather (d. 2019) guide the record "like an ʻaumākua… a
  Hawaiian ancestral spirit guide that manifests in different forms." —
  https://daily.bandcamp.com/features/kalia-vandever-we-fell-in-turn-interview
- On the solo/ambient turn: "Once the pandemic hit, I delved into my solo
  process even more and found a closeness with the sonic landscape, as well as
  the meditative nature of ambient music." —
  https://www.flowstate.fm/p/kalia-vandever-interview

## Confidence

**Solid:** the track is explicitly about her parents' separation and the *choice
of how to move through pain* (her own words, Bandcamp Daily); album-level
dream/ʻaumākua/Hawaiian-ancestry framing and the loop-over-drone "trom-drone"
method are well-corroborated across NPR and Bandcamp. **Thin:** no full liner
note or standalone artist statement dedicated solely to "Temper The Wound" was
found — the deepest track-specific quote is the single Bandcamp Daily interview
line; the emotional/sonic "spirals upward into safety" read is partly critic
(Huizenga/NPR), not the artist verbatim.
