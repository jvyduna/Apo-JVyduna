# "Bliss" by Ducky — Artist Vision & Intent Research

## Synthesis

Ducky is Morgan Neiman, an electronic producer/DJ (LA-based, later associated
with Toronto). "Bliss" is a 2016 single released on **Secret Songs**, the
curated singles series run by producer Ryan Hemsworth, with an official video
that premiered on Pitchfork. The track sits in Ducky's signature fast, melodic,
emotionally-charged "rave" lane — closer to **~150 BPM** than the seed's stated
160 BPM (multiple write-ups peg their signature tracks, Bliss included, at 150).
Crucially: **no track-specific interview, liner note, or press quote from Ducky
about the meaning of "Bliss" was found.** What exists is their broader,
well-documented artistic vision — fast, joyful, un-cynical, community-oriented
music that carries genuine emotion at high tempo. Findings below therefore lean
on artist/scene-level statements, clearly flagged as such.

## Findings (each with source)

- **Release / label**: "Bliss" released **Aug 12, 2016** on **Secret Songs**
  (Ryan Hemsworth's curated singles imprint). Bandcamp lists it under a
  "Don't Give Up Yet" grouping; tagged Toronto.
  https://shhsecretsongs.bandcamp.com/track/bliss
- **Video / premiere**: Official "Bliss" music video premiered on **Pitchfork
  (~July 29, 2016)**; directed by Alexander Girav & Ross Constable, shot/edited
  by Alexander Girav. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy1VZavSUFQ
- **Tempo & character (secondary characterization, not Ducky's own words)**:
  Insomniac describes Bliss and peer tracks ("Pokemon Wars," "Work," "Winter
  Song") as **"carefree adventurousness at 150 BPM."**
  https://www.insomniac.com/magazine/my-charming-ducky-youre-for-me/
- **Artist identity**: Ducky = **Morgan Neiman**, LA-based producer; their
  "160 BPM (and rising)" sets drew Skrillex, Anna Lunoe, Mija, Fan Fiction.
  https://relentlessbeats.com/2021/08/dj-to-watch-ducky/
- **Vision — fast music as warmth/joy (Ducky's own words, artist-level)**:
  "Honestly it's the nicest scene I've ever participated in... genuinely nice
  and enthusiastic kids on the internet who like to make and hear fast music"
  (on nightcore); advises producers "don't feel limited or defined by genres";
  keeps a rotating 300+ track playlist spanning **100–216 BPM**.
  https://dmy.co/features/ducky-nightcore-interview
- **Namesake / disambiguation**: NOT Tyla's "Bliss" (Wikipedia), nor the
  various indie/rock "Bliss" tracks. Also distinct from Ducky's own DJ mix
  **"BLISS TAPE 01"** (SoundCloud) — a mixtape, not this single.
  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bliss_(Tyla_song) ·
  https://soundcloud.com/ducky/bliss-tape-01

## Confidence

- **Solid**: Artist identity (Morgan Neiman); "Bliss" is a 2016 Secret
  Songs / Ryan Hemsworth single with a Pitchfork-premiered video; Ducky's
  general aesthetic is fast (100–216 BPM range), melodic, joyful, community-
  minded, anti-genre-purism.
- **Thin / caveat**: **No primary statement by Ducky specifically about
  "Bliss"** was located — themes/intent for this exact track are inferred from
  album- and artist-level material. Tempo is reported as **~150 BPM** by
  secondary sources, conflicting with the seed's 160 BPM (unverified against a
  measured source); treat BPM as approximate.
