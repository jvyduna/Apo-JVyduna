# Opus No 1 — Subjective Listener Reactions

Research focus: how ordinary listeners describe and feel about "Opus No. 1,"
the Cisco default hold music. Track: composed 1989 by Tim Carleton (with
Darrick Deel on a 4-track); Deel later worked at Cisco and got it adopted as
the default IP-phone hold music (now on 65M+ devices). Carleton earned "not a
penny" — licensing was an informal favor, not a commercial deal, which makes
the story ripe for a fun non-commercial art-use ask. Distinct from any classical
"Opus No. 1" namesakes: this is the synth/drum-machine hold-music track.

## Synthesis of the "feel"

Reactions are overwhelmingly, disproportionately affectionate for utilitarian
background music. Recurring emotional register: **nostalgic, optimistic,
transcendent/surreal, comforting, and unexpectedly funky/danceable**. The
dominant imagery is **liminal corporate-futurism** — a "training video for a
software company that never launched" — which listeners explicitly tie to
**vaporwave nostalgia**. A second cluster is quasi-religious/absurdist awe ("God
put me on hold"). People report *wanting* to be on hold, and treat a purely
functional cue as a beloved song worth hunting down.

## Findings (each with source)

- Iconic top comment, widely quoted: **"It feels like God put me on hold."**
  Transcendent/surreal register. — https://roadtonowhere.forumotion.org/t273-tim-carleton-darrick-deel-opus-no-1-a-k-a-cisco-hold-music
- Vaporwave / liminal framing: it's "what Vaporwave is nostalgic for" and "feels
  like the soundtrack to a training video for a futuristic software company that
  never actually launched." Descriptors: "weird, driving energy," "optimistic,"
  "a bit 80s." — https://locotheme.com/why-the-cisco-systems-hold-music-is-unironically-a-masterpiece-2511
- Vivid standout-moment imagery at ~2:53: "a shimmering segment that feels like
  a prince dancing up a crystal staircase and then sliding down the banister with
  his head flung back in a moment of bliss." Reviewer also calls it "a warm shirt
  out of the dryer" and "crying at the movie on a first date." — https://holdmusicreviews.wordpress.com/2021/01/08/opus-no-1-cisco-default-hold-music/
- Wanting to stay on hold: "So addictively pleasant. Please, put me back on hold
  so I can listen to it some more" and "Best hold music ever made. I love this
  song." — https://www.replicant.com/blog/the-surprising-roots-of-one-of-the-most-common-hold-music-songs
- Danceable/funky read: forum poster "grooving to its strange synth sounds,"
  finds it "pretty funky" for corporate hold music. A Facebook post: "honestly if
  they played this at the club I'd get down." — https://roadtonowhere.forumotion.org/t273-tim-carleton-darrick-deel-opus-no-1-a-k-a-cisco-hold-music
  ; https://www.facebook.com/officialnicolestorydent/posts/533481019731596/
- Obsessive devotion documented on *This American Life*: a listener kept detailed
  notes each time he heard it; another (Denise Carter Stanley) listens "sometimes
  for more than an hour a day." Comedic melancholy in YouTube comments: "This song
  is the anthem of my unemployment." — https://holdmusicreviews.wordpress.com/2021/01/08/opus-no-1-cisco-default-hold-music/
  ; https://www.replicant.com/blog/the-surprising-roots-of-one-of-the-most-common-hold-music-songs
- Composer's own reaction (bemused, embarrassed): hearing it unexpectedly, "I
  just start blushing immediately." Useful tone for an art-use ask — he treats
  the cult status warmly, not proprietarily. — https://www.replicant.com/blog/the-surprising-roots-of-one-of-the-most-common-hold-music-songs

## Confidence

**Solid:** the emotional consensus (nostalgic, optimistic, comforting,
surreally transcendent, secretly funky) and the vaporwave/liminal framing —
these recur across independent sources (blogs, forum, corporate-blog interview,
This American Life recap). The "God put me on hold" line and the composer/no-
royalties backstory are well-corroborated. **Thin:** exact comment wording and
attribution (many quotes are paraphrased through secondary blogs, not scraped
directly from YouTube/Reddit); the ~2:53 timestamp is one reviewer's read of one
upload, not canonical; I could not open Reddit or RYM threads directly, so the
"Reddit consensus" is inferred from aggregators rather than primary threads.
