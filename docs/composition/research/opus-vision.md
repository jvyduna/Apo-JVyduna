# Opus No 1 — Artist Vision & Intent

## Synthesis

"Opus No. 1" is the near-ubiquitous default hold music on Cisco IP phones,
composed in **1989 by Tim Carleton (then 16) with his friend Darrick Deel**,
recorded on a 4-track in Carleton's parents' California garage using a DX7-era
synth and drum machine, in a "Yanni-loving computer nerd" idiom. There is **no
grand artistic vision statement** here — the honest, well-sourced truth is that
it was teenage bedroom/garage noodling that lay forgotten for a decade until
Deel, by then a Cisco engineer, pitched it as phone hold music. In interviews
Carleton comes across as **bemused and mildly embarrassed** by its fame rather
than mission-driven. The interesting narrative for an art piece is the
accidental ubiquity: a throwaway teenage synth jam that became one of the
most-heard pieces of music on Earth (65M+ devices). Track-specific "meaning"
material is thin; what exists is anecdote and origin story, noted below.

## Findings (each with source)

- Composed 1989 by Tim Carleton (age 16) and Darrick Deel; recorded on a 4-track
  in the garage; described as a "Yanni-loving computer nerd messing around with a
  drum machine and a synthesizer." — This American Life (2014), summarized in
  Replicant. https://www.replicant.com/blog/the-surprising-roots-of-one-of-the-most-common-hold-music-songs
- Sat forgotten ~a decade until Deel joined Cisco and pitched it for the new IP
  phone system: *"I've got this piece of music that I know sounds really good on
  the phone."* — NBC Bay Area feature, via Replicant (above) and search summaries.
- Now embedded in ~65 million Cisco IP phones. — Mental Floss.
  https://www.mentalfloss.com/article/68163/ciscos-default-hold-music-was-written-16-year-old-computer-nerd
- Carleton's own tone on its success: *"Not a penny... I didn't make any money
  from my music,"* and describes the eerie embarrassment of unexpectedly hearing
  his own creation. — This American Life (2014), via Replicant.
- **Ownership/licensing (key for use requests):** Ownership was *never*
  transferred to Cisco. **Carleton retains 100%**; Cisco licenses it
  **non-exclusively** from him. It has since been licensed for **sync in TV,
  film, and streaming**, and featured in a **2023 Bud Light Super Bowl ad**. —
  Global News. https://globalnews.ca/news/10416687/on-hold-music-history/
- Primary source is This American Life ep. **516 "Stuck in the Middle" (2014)**,
  reported by Sara Corbett — the origin of nearly all quotes (transcript pages
  returned HTTP 403 to automated fetch; cited secondhand).
  https://www.thisamericanlife.org/553/transcript
- Distinct-artist note: the credited artist is **Tim Carleton & Darrick Deel**
  (Spotify artist/track pages exist); do not confuse with the many SoundCloud
  remixes/bootlegs or unrelated "Opus No. 1" tracks (Camille Laddawan, GammaFlow).
  https://open.spotify.com/track/1NWBPylvcRabSGJioO0go1
