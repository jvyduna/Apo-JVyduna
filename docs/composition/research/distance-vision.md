# "Our Distance" (Helios) — Artist Vision & Intent

## Synthesis

"Our Distance" is a track on **Domicile** (Helios, released Dec 11, 2020, on
Ghostly International). Helios is **Keith Kenniff** (also Goldmund, Mint Julep;
married collaborator Hollie Kenniff) — the correct artist, distinct from the
Greek sun-god namesake and from unrelated "Helios" acts. No source found gives a
track-specific statement about "Our Distance" itself; the seed title "Our
Distance and That of Trees" appears to be a conflation and is **not** a real
title in Kenniff's discography (Domicile's neighboring tracks include "Their
Places," "Domain," "One And The Same"). All artist-stated vision below is
therefore **album-level** for Domicile, which is the correct interpretive frame.

Domicile's core intent: intentional ambient music for the home as sanctuary —
calm, non-demanding, reframing "background music" as something deliberately
compositional in the lineage of Brian Eno's ambient. Written before the pandemic
but released into lockdown, Kenniff framed it as a judgement-free "safe space."

## Findings

- **Track/album placement**: "Our Distance" is on Domicile, released Dec 11,
  2020; artist Helios = Keith Kenniff + Hollie Kenniff. No track-level liner
  notes or description on the official page.
  [Bandcamp track](https://heliosmusic.bandcamp.com/track/our-distance) ·
  [Bandcamp album](https://heliosmusic.bandcamp.com/album/domicile)

- **Stated intent (Kenniff, verbatim)**: "I wanted to write an album that
  inspired calmness and reflection; something specifically designed to be
  listened to at home, either alone or within the closeness of our inner
  circles."
  [Stationary Travels review quoting Kenniff](https://stationarytravels.wordpress.com/2021/01/16/a-sense-of-place-domicile-by-helios/)

- **Pandemic reframing (Kenniff, verbatim)**: "As we are all currently subject
  to lives of lockdowns, intermittent flux, and general philosophical upheaval,
  my hope is that this music can be a safe space to go to without judgement or
  expectation, without anxiety or stress."
  [Stationary Travels](https://stationarytravels.wordpress.com/2021/01/16/a-sense-of-place-domicile-by-helios/)

- **On stepping back (Kenniff, verbatim)**: "Sometimes time and space can reframe
  the way we see things and if we step back for a moment perhaps we will be able
  to step forward again with more purpose and determination."
  [Stationary Travels](https://stationarytravels.wordpress.com/2021/01/16/a-sense-of-place-domicile-by-helios/)

- **Ambient philosophy / imagery**: label press frames each piece as designed to
  "gently color the day or accompany an activity," akin to Eno's original
  declaration of ambient; the record "dwells in that day-to-day sanctuary, the
  space between concrete instances and events," with sounds lingering like "the
  way a window's light or a tree's shadow moves across the wall" — the only
  tree/light-shadow imagery found, and it is album-level, not about this track.
  [Bandcamp album](https://heliosmusic.bandcamp.com/album/domicile) ·
  [Ghostly artist page](https://ghostly.com/artists/helios)

- **Method (general Helios practice, from interviews)**: Kenniff favors
  "accidental" found sounds mangled via mini-cassette re-recording (a floor creak
  becomes a hi-hat, a house crack a snare) — his broad Helios/Eingya method, not
  documented specifically for Domicile or this track.
  [FACT interview 2015](https://www.factmag.com/2015/08/31/helios-keith-kenniff-interview/)

## Confidence

- **Solid**: artist identity (Kenniff, not namesakes); album/label/date; the
  three verbatim Kenniff quotes on Domicile's intent (home, calm, lockdown safe
  space); the Eno-lineage ambient framing and the window-light/tree-shadow image.
- **Thin/absent**: anything specific to "Our Distance" as a track — no interview,
  liner note, or post addresses it individually. The "…and That of Trees" title
  is unverified and likely erroneous. Use album-level intent as the proxy.
