# "Rafters" — Lusine — Artist Vision / Intent

## Synthesis

The context seed was wrong on the album. **"Rafters" is not on _Sensorimotor_ (2017).** It is
**track 10 on _Long Light_ (Ghostly International, GI-427, released Sept 15, 2023)**, the 9th
LP by **Lusine = Jeff McIlwain**, a Seattle-based electronic producer (distinct from the
1990s Swiss project "L'usine" on Discogs). No track-specific statement from McIlwain about
"Rafters" exists in available sources — it is a wordless, purely instrumental piece and gets
only passing mention in press. So the artist's *stated* vision must be read at the **album
level**, where it is documented, plus reviewers' sonic characterizations of the track itself.

Rights path: Ghostly International (confirmed — the label released and hosts the album).

## Findings

- **Album, not single.** "Rafters" is track 10 on _Long Light_ (2023), Lusine's 9th album,
  marking ~20 years on Ghostly. — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sensorimotor_(album) (rules out Sensorimotor);
  https://lusine.bandcamp.com/album/long-light ; https://ghostly.com/products/long-light
- **Artist ID / namesake.** Lusine = Jeff McIlwain, Seattle; catalog on Discogs is separate
  from the older Swiss "L'usine." — https://www.discogs.com/artist/2404-Lusine
- **Album's stated theme (McIlwain, direct):** contemporary anxiety and unstable perception —
  *"There's this sort of paranoia where you don't know what is real, it's an age of high
  anxiety and there are all these distractions,"* a *"fun house mirror situation."* —
  https://inverted-audio.com/lusine-lines-long-light-ghostly-international/
- **Title metaphor:** "Long Light" comes from collaborator Benoît Pioulard's lyric *"long
  light signalling the fall again"* — a guiding image of seasonal turn / fading light. —
  https://inverted-audio.com/lusine-lines-long-light-ghostly-international/
- **Process, in his words:** *"Music making is a struggle and you have to have a ton of
  patience"*; he builds tracks from a single core element (a vocal cut or beat) outward,
  keeping textures *"dynamic yet minimalist as ever."* —
  https://inverted-audio.com/lusine-lines-long-light-ghostly-international/ ;
  https://xlr8r.com/news/lusine-is-back-with-a-new-album/
- **Where "Rafters" sits in the album:** press frames it as one of the record's
  *"ambient-minded foreshadowing"* instrumental pieces (grouped with "Faceless" and
  "Plateau") — i.e. a quieter, tension-building interlude vs. the vocal-pop tracks. —
  https://inverted-audio.com/lusine-lines-long-light-ghostly-international/
- **Reviewer sonic read of the track:** described as having a *"fluted urgency and eerie
  looped train-track sample"* — a nervy, mechanical, looping texture. — search snippet from
  https://hearfeel.co.uk/2023/11/07/lusine-long-light/

## Confidence

- **Solid:** correct album/track placement, release/label/date, artist identity vs. namesake,
  and the album-level intent quotes (anxiety / "fun house mirror," title metaphor, minimalist
  looping process) — all from primary/press sources tied to the 2023 release.
- **Thin:** anything specific to *"Rafters"* itself. No interview or liner note discusses this
  track directly; it is instrumental, so no lyric intent. The "train-track sample / fluted
  urgency" line is a single reviewer's characterization, not the artist's words. Treat
  track-level meaning as inferred from album context, not stated.
