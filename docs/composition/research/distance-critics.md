# "Our Distance" (Helios) — Critic Impressions

## Synthesis

**Identity correction first.** The seed's alternate title "Our Distance and That of
Trees" does not exist in the Helios catalog. "Our Distance" is **track 3 on _Domicile_
(Ghostly International, 11 Dec 2020)** — Helios's second Ghostly LP, after _Veriditas_
(2018). Helios is indeed **Keith Kenniff** (also Goldmund, Mint Julep, Harbors; wife
Hollie Kenniff contributes), an ambient/post-classical composer who also scores film.
Do not confuse with: the band God Is An Astronaut's album _Helios_, Orvis Helios fly
rods, the _Helios_ novel/film, or the Symphonium Helios IEM — all surfaced in searches.

**On the critic ask:** there is essentially **no professional review of the individual
track** "Our Distance." Critical impressions attach to the parent album _Domicile_, and
even that got thin major-outlet coverage (no Pitchfork/Quietus/RA/PopMatters review
found for _Domicile_). The strongest, most-cited Helios critical writing is for a
*different* album, _Eingya_ (2006) — useful for the artist's aesthetic vocabulary but
not this track.

## Findings

- _Domicile_ concept: a "domestic ambient" record — calm synth tones/textures meant to
  "serve the inside of a home," reframing background music as intentional; framed as
  Eno's _Music for Airports_ "transposed to a domestic context." — Stationary Travels,
  https://stationarytravels.wordpress.com/2021/01/16/a-sense-of-place-domicile-by-helios/
- _Domicile_ sound (same review): "somber organ tones slowly swell, undulate, and fan
  out"; "unhurried," "unobtrusive and unstructured"; "abstract distillations of the
  essence of home" evoking "everything is going to be okay." No per-track note on "Our
  Distance." — https://stationarytravels.wordpress.com/2021/01/16/a-sense-of-place-domicile-by-helios/
- Aggregated reception for _Domicile_ is mixed-to-warm; a recurring criticism is
  sameness/monotony over ~40 min ("great ideas and drone textures, but... the same
  thing over and over"). Listener reception (Bandcamp) skews positive: "beautiful,
  calming and epic." — https://www.albumoftheyear.org/album/291287-helios-domicile.php ;
  https://heliosmusic.bandcamp.com/album/domicile
- Track/album facts: "Our Distance" is a digital track on _Domicile_; genre tags
  ambient/electronic/IDM; no liner notes or press quotes for the track itself on the
  official page. — https://heliosmusic.bandcamp.com/track/our-distance
- Artist aesthetic vocabulary (from acclaimed _Eingya_, 2006, Type/Unseen): Pitchfork
  characterized Helios as "hazy ambience" balancing "washed-out electronic processing
  with clear guitar melodies and delicate piano"; Tiny Mix Tapes and Sputnikmusic
  (4.5/5) frame it as nostalgic, soothing, cinematic post-rock-ambient. Applies to the
  artist, not this track. — https://www.tinymixtapes.com/music-review/helios-eingya ;
  https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/56489/Helios-Eingya/
- Bio corroboration: Kenniff = Helios/Goldmund; ambient + post-classical + film scoring.
  — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keith_Kenniff ; https://www.factmag.com/2015/08/31/helios-keith-kenniff-interview/

## Confidence

- **Solid:** track identity/placement (Domicile, 2020, Ghostly, track 3); seed's
  alt-title is a phantom; artist identity and namesake disambiguation; _Domicile_'s
  domestic-ambient concept and general mood descriptors.
- **Thin:** no dedicated professional critique of the *track* "Our Distance" exists;
  _Domicile_ itself was lightly reviewed by top-tier outlets (relied on a blog +
  aggregator). The Pitchfork/TMT/Sputnik language describes the artist via _Eingya_,
  not this song — use as aesthetic backdrop only, not as commentary on "Our Distance."
