# "Bliss" by Ducky — Critical Impressions

## Synthesis

"Bliss" is the opening track of Ducky's four-track EP **Don't Give Up Yet**
(Secret Songs, Ryan Hemsworth's label; released **August 12, 2016**). Ducky is
**Morgan Neiman**, an LA-based producer/singer/DJ (NYU music-production grad) —
not to be confused with namesakes like the band Charly Bliss, the Wiggles'
"Ducky Ducky," or MUST DIE!'s "Bliss 2K." There is **no coverage from the
tier-1 critical outlets** the task hoped for (Pitchfork, The Quietus, Resident
Advisor, Tiny Mix Tapes, AllMusic, PopMatters) — none reviewed this track or EP.
The usable critical characterization comes from dance/electronic press
(Insomniac Magazine, Vice/Noisey) and a dedicated music blog. Consistent theme:
buoyant, high-energy, joyful club-pop with a personal/emotional arc.

## Findings

- Opening-track praise: "the ultimate opening track, immediately catching your
  ear, while setting the scene for the joyous journey through sound." —
  Enlightenment For Your Ears blog review.
  https://enlightenmentforyourears.blogspot.com/2016/08/ducky-dont-give-up-yet-ep.html
- Sound of "Bliss": "a peppy, high-spirited track with supersonic synth strides,
  centered around punchy percussion pops and rising bass bumps." — same review.
- EP framing: "Buoyant, bouncy and fantastically freeing, each track irrupts
  with unfamiliarly fresh feels"; the EP "sets a new, sparkling, standard both
  for her productions as well as Secret Songs'." — same review.
- Structural read: the EP reviewed as "an artistic arc of musical movement,"
  not just similar-sounding tracks; closer "Open Yourself Up and Let Her Pour
  Out" strips back to a "more peeled back, emotional nature." — same review.
- Tempo/energy characterization: Insomniac ties Ducky's "carefree
  adventurousness at **150 BPM**" directly to tracks including "Bliss" (also
  "Pokemon Wars," "Work," "Winter Song").
  https://www.insomniac.com/magazine/my-charming-ducky-youre-for-me/
- Broader critical style (Vice/Noisey): "super-chill, weird-wonderful pop, full
  of unexpected grooves, interstellar bleeps"; captures "the freshness of
  falling in love, but also the blissful comfort" of deep connection.
  https://www.vice.com/en/article/rgpyzb/premiere-ducky-you-on-top-ep
- Artist self-description (same Vice piece): can "write a club track, and a pop
  song, and something weird and broken and downtempo, and they all sound like
  me."
- Thematic context: EP is about "finally finding hope after a lifelong battle
  with undiagnosed bipolar and trauma" — hope/resilience frame.
  https://shhsecretsongs.bandcamp.com/album/dont-give-up-yet

## Confidence

- **Solid:** release facts (date, label, EP, artist identity), the blog's
  track-level description of "Bliss," and the general "joyful/buoyant club-pop"
  critical consensus.
- **Thin / caution:** The seed's **"160 BPM"** is unverified — the strongest
  source (Insomniac) associates "Bliss" with **150 BPM**; treat tempo as
  ~150–160, confirm by ear. No major-outlet (Pitchfork/RA/Quietus/AllMusic)
  reviews exist, so "professional critic" impressions rest mostly on one
  enthusiast blog plus dance-press features, not marquee criticism.
