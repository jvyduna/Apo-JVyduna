- **Duration:** 3:45 (224.93 s).

  estimator; treat both as loose — the music has no steady pulse.)
- **Instrumentation:** solo trombone looped/delayed into "trom-drone" — whirling
  melodies over a drone of luminous air, plus voice and pandemic-era effects
  pedals. Stems: one melodic `other` (trombone, 706 note events, range A#1–G5),
  no real drums (the `snare` stem is empty).
- **Arc position:** the **genesis**. This is birth — *being birthed into a
  personal religion.* Jeff's one-liner: "Hopeful, childbirth, mournful, healing
  through a difficult period." It has to open the whole 20-minute journey by
  establishing the world softly, from near-nothing, and carry the emotional beat
  of *a difficult beginning that heals as it rises* — the same shape the music
  enacts (a falling-dream that spirals gently upward into safety).

## 3. Reception & meaning

Three lenses, synthesized. Confidence is high on identity/genre/concept, lower on
marquee-critic depth (this is a niche ambient/jazz release; no Pitchfork/RA/Quietus
review of the track exists, and grassroots listener chatter is thin).

**How listeners feel it.** A remarkably tight consensus around *sadness that
consoles*. Two poles recur — grief and floating dreamlike peace — with the
sadness framed as welcome and healing rather than bleak: "the saddest trombone
I've heard in many years… melancholic beauty," "a weapon of profound sadness and
grieving," yet "the soundtrack to a beautiful goodbye, the kind of sadness you're
grateful to have experienced" and "dreamlike and comforting, a woven shawl of
sound." It reads as a genuine standout — last.fm shows it the **2nd-most-played
track** on the album (2,672 listeners). Touchstones people reach for: Grouper,
Basinski's *Disintegration Loops*, Pharoah Sanders/Floating Points.
(https://kaliavandever.bandcamp.com/album/we-fell-in-turn ·
https://www.last.fm/music/Kalia+Vandever/We+Fell+in+Turn ·
https://acloserlisten.com/2023/03/28/kalia-vandever-we-fell-in-turn/)

**Artist's stated vision.** Vandever says the track is about **her parents'
separation at the end of middle school** — and specifically about *"how you decide
to move through that pain,"* not the pain itself. A healing/processing arc. The
album's engine is dream imagery — the sensation of *falling right before waking*
(source of the title) — threaded through her Hawaiian ancestry and *ʻaumākua*,
ancestral spirit guides that appear in dreams and surface childhood memory. Method:
improvised solo trombone + voice + looping/delay pedals layered over a luminous
drone. (https://daily.bandcamp.com/features/kalia-vandever-we-fell-in-turn-interview
· https://www.kalw.org/2023-02-03/kalia-vandever-temper-the-wound)

**Critics' take.** Converge on *ambient / dreamlike / looping / comforting-yet-
sorrowful*. Stereogum: "gorgeous, searching ambient music" (Grouper, Basinski).
NPR's Tom Huizenga coins **"trom-drone"** — the trombone "interleaves loops with
gentle, whirling melodies that circle above a drone of luminous air" and "seems to
spiral delicately upward to a safer space." Brainwashed singles the track out as
balancing **"sadness with strength and survival."**
(https://stereogum.com/2212686/kalia-vandever-temper-the-wound/music ·
https://www.npr.org/sections/now-playing/2023/02/03/1153888512/kalia-vandever-temper-the-wound
· https://brainwashed.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=32716)

**Takeaway for the visuals:** the emotional target is *warm mournful hope that
rises* — not bleak, not tech, not busy. A luminous drifting field that a difficult
memory blooms through and then dissolves. Falling-dream → spiral upward → safe.

## 5. Artistic feedback & direction

**Frame the whole piece from here.** This is the birth and the establishing shot
of a 20-minute story, following dead silence and opening the room. It should feel
like *emerging into a warm, luminous fog* — the "drone of luminous air" made
visible. Everything about it must contrast hard with **its neighbor, `bliss`**
(song 2: 160 BPM rigid, CMY/Rubik tech, After Dark screensavers). Temper is the
antithesis: slow, rubato, breathing, pastel, organic, no hard edges, no beat grid.
If a viewer can tap their foot to it, we've failed.

**The exterior/interior split is the strongest structural idea Jeff already has,
and it's thematically perfect.** Keep the **cube interior a dark womb** and let the
world manifest only on the **exterior faces + cylinder ring**. Birth = something
appearing on the outside while the inside stays a held, protected dark. Reveal the
interior only at the very end, or never.

**Map the visuals to the music's own falling-then-rising shape.** Vertical motion
is the whole language: the melody "spirals upward to a safer space," and the MIR
literally climbs — highest notes at **1:09.8 (D6)** and **2:32.6 (G5)**. Let
brightness, figures, or light-strands *rise* on the walls. Drive their height and
glow from the trombone (`other`) envelope, not a beat.

**Three anchors to choreograph to (all opinionated, all concrete):**
1. **0:40 inhale valley (energy 0.05, Gm→E7 shadow).** Drop nearly to black — the
   first wound, a held breath before the first cry. Don't fill it; earn the
   emptiness. Then bloom back as G returns at 0:54.9.
2. **2:32–2:45 climax (peak 0.78, G→D→G, G5 apex).** The birth bloom / full
   emotional open. This is where whatever hero pattern is doing gets its brightest,
   fullest, highest moment. Everything the piece has been building toward.
3. **3:18→3:41 dissolve (Bm turn → Ebaug, energy 0.04).** Let it decay Basinski-
   style — loops crumbling, light smearing and dimming to a single unresolved
   held breath, then black. The augmented final chord should feel *unresolved on
   purpose* (falling right before waking) — do NOT tie a bow on it. That
   unresolved hang is the hook into song 2.

**Lean into:** pastel warmth (Try Radish mint/sky/cream, or Gareth sky/lavender/
peach); slow feedback/smear (the gauzy gossamer loop quality); upward drift;
sparse, deliberate reveals timed to chord changes; near-black negative space as an
active choice. **Avoid:** rigid beat sync, geometric/tech screensaver motifs
(save those for bliss), saturated primaries, fast strobe, anything busy or
"designed." Restraint is the point — this is the quietest, most patient the whole
piece will be.

**Standing rule (2026-07-08): all Temper patterns are super chill and the
recording is not on a fixed tempo grid — no sudden jumps, ever.** No tempo
gating (Sync/TempoDiv), no Meta/random-jump triggers, no drift/re-randomize
triggers, no audio reactivity in Temper patterns; everything free-runs and all
choreography is placed on the arrange timeline by hand.

## 6. Pattern & effect plan

Ranked. One hero for first-pass implementation.

1. **⭐ HERO — "Luminous Words" (REUSE `Text` + `BlitFeedback`).** Jeff's Idea 1,
   and by far the fastest path to a working, on-theme opener. Ghost **HEAL /
   BIRTH / HOPE** out of a pastel drone fog on the **exterior faces only**;
   interior stays black (womb). Each word emerges on a chord-change anchor, dwells
   through the drone, then dissolves via `BlitFeedback` feedback smear (the
   "gauzy, gossamer" trombone-loop quality made literal). Brightness tracks the
   trombone envelope; the word barely resolves at the 0:40 valley, reaches full
   legibility/glow at the **2:32–2:45 climax**, and smears to black in the
   **3:18→3:41 dissolve**. `Text` already exists (`patterns/Text.java`, 5px font,
   exterior view support); `BlitFeedback` already exists. Triggers: chord-change
   onsets, section boundaries, the energy valley/peak — easily ≥3. Low build risk,
   high narrative payoff. Ship this first.

2. **"Aumākua" cave-painting family tree (NEW class).** Jeff's Idea 2 and the
   signature centerpiece for a second pass — nothing in inventory does it. A
   generator drawing cave-painting human figures that scroll slowly upward; limbs
   drip into new figures, weaving lineage/family-tree branches. Deeply on-theme
   (childbirth, ancestry, *ʻaumākua*). Anchor spawns to the upward register moments
   (new figure blooms at 1:09.8 and the 2:32 climax); figures dissolve in the
   3:18→3:41 fade. Heavier build (generative figure system, drip physics, zero-

3. **Mystify reskin (REUSE `Mystify`).** The "gentle whirling melody circling
   above a drone" almost verbatim — a slow-drifting polygon, but pastel, low
   speed, brightness/vertex-drift driven by the trombone envelope (Audio depth
   default 0 = pure screensaver). Excellent *companion drone layer* under the hero
   on the cylinder ring or as a low-glow wash. Confirm its motion cap stays under
   the ≥5 s traversal rule at these slow speeds.

4. **UnknownPleasures reskin (REUSE `UnknownPleasures`).** Stacked ridgelines as a
   breathing pastel horizon = the "woven shawl of sound." Ridge height driven by
   the mix/trombone envelope so the horizon swells at 1:00/2:06 and flattens at the
   0:40 valley and the outro. Good quiet background texture; keep it dim.

5. **Terraform wash (REUSE `Terraform`).** Slow morphing luminous topography as the
   base "drone of luminous air" — a barely-moving pastel underlay beneath the hero
   words. Lowest layer; near-static, low brightness.

6. **BlitFeedback as connective tissue (REUSE effect).** Beyond the hero, a light
   feedback smear over any layer sells the loop/delay aesthetic and powers the
   Basinski-style crumble in the 3:18→3:41 dissolve. Reusable across all layers.

**Explicitly avoid for this song:** `Outrun`, `Pipes3D`, `Rubik`, `Zot`,
`Satori`, `MirPitchGrid`, and the `Rip` effect — these are the tech/geometric/
percussive vocabulary of **bliss**. Using them here would blur the birth-vs-
machine contrast that gives the arc its shape. `MirPitchGrid` in particular reads
as a grid/light-organ (wrong for a rubato drone) despite the melodic stem being
available; skip it.
